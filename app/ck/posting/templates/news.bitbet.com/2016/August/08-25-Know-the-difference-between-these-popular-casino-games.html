Single hand vs. Switch blackjack
The difference between these two games is pretty much in the name. Single hand blackjack is the standard game of 21, in which the player tries to draw a hand closest to 21 to beat the dealer’s hand without going over 21.

Switch blackjack actually deals out two hands versus the dealer’s one, and allows players to swap out their top cards in order to create better hands. Some rules are also different in switch blackjack. The dealer is awarded a push if they draw 22 – rather than bust like in single hand – and a player blackjack pays even money instead of the standard casino payout.

Pai Gow poker vs. Let It Ride poker
Poker is one of the most popular casino games with multiple variations and two of the more common twists are Pai Gow and Let It Ride. Pai Gow deals the player seven cards which are broken into a five-card poker hand and a two-card hand. The dealer is also dealt seven cards to make similar hands. The player or dealer must win both hands to collect.

Let It Ride poker has the player places three bets of the same size before the cards are dealt. Then there are three cards dealt face up to the player, who can either withdraw their pending wagers or “Let It Ride” to see the next two cards – depending on the strength of those first three cards. Instead of playing against the dealer, you are trying to hit high-ranked poker hands that have corresponding payouts.


American Roulette vs. European Roulette
American Roulette is the standard game you see in most casinos and on television. Players can wager on the outcome of the spin, with different numbers, colors and combinations paying out different sizes. Two of those options are the double zero and single zero wagers, which both pay 35/1 odds. In European Roulette, the double zero is gone, leaving only the single zero option.
