Know the subtle difference in these popular casino games

With so many casino games to choose from, it’s tough to know the differences


Casino patrons – online and in brick and mortar joints – have more options than ever when it comes to the gaming menu. Bettors looking for some free bitcoin can pick and choose from a long list of offerings, from traditional casino contests to innovative twists on these classics. 
 
But with so many casino games to choose from, it’s tough to know the differences. Here’s what to look for. 
 
Six-deck Blackjack vs. Single-deck Blackjack 
The difference is basically in the name, however, there are some twists to each game that make them very unique. First, six-deck blackjack is your standard fare blackjack: dealer stands on 17 and deals on 16, insurance pays 2/1, blackjack pays 3/2, pushes refund your wagers. Single-deck blackjack, on the other hand, has its own set of stipulations for the 52-card game. Bet limits can often times be lower as is the payout for blackjack, dropping to 6/5. 
 
American Roulette vs. European Roulette 
Wheel spins. Ball goes in. Bounce, bounce, bounce. That’s pretty simple. Roulette is an easy game to figure out, with a long list of wagering options. You have two different takes on the game, American and European, which really aren’t all that different. American roulette offers another green pocket with a double zero while European roulette has just the single zero pocket. This added green space actually gives the house a better edge over the player, so if you like to play the odds, European roulette may be your flavor of choice. 
 
Casino Hold’em vs. Let It Ride 
Poker perhaps has the most variations on the casino floor, with a number of takes on the popular game. Two of the more popular options are Casino Hold’em and Let It Ride, which share some similarities in terms of wagering but play out by different rules. Casino Hold’em takes its cues from Texas Hold’em, dealing out two cards each and playing community cards. You must place an ante bet to start (with three community cards the dealt) and must place a call bet to reveal the final two cards. In Let It Ride, you make an initial ante bet and are dealt three cards, then have the option to raise by the same amount based on how strong your poker hand is.
