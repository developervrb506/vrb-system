Three strategies for playing and winning at roulette



  Tweet

One of the simplest ways to attack the roulette table is to bet the board


Roulette is one of the classic games that come to mind when you think about playing in a bitcoin casino, whether that’s in the bright lights of Las Vegas or in the comfort of your own home with an online casino like BitBet.com. There are numerous ways to play and bet the game, but here are three simple strategies that can help you win when the ball stops bouncing:

Play the board
One of the simplest ways to attack the roulette table is to bet the board. Most roulette games will have a constantly-updating board which shows players which numbers and colors have hit in recent games. Players like to monitor the board and wager what color is due or which one is on a hot run. These are even-money wagers, paying 1:1, which means a $20 winning wager on black will pay out $20.

Slow play
For those players ready to commit some time to the game, a slow play option using only wagers of 2:1 odds can be a profitable system. One of the more popular approaches is the play the dozen bets. With numbers 1 through 36 on the inside of the board, players can bet on whether the ball will land within the first dozen numbers (1-12), the second dozen (13-24) or the third dozen (25-36).

Because these types of wagers pay 2:1 odds, players will often times wager the same amount on two of the three dozen bets, knowing that they will lose one but have a two in three chance of winning double their amount. If a player takes the first and third dozen with a $30 wager on each, and it lands on No. 6, they win $60 for the first dozen and lose the initial $30 bet on the third dozen, but are still up $30 from that one game. The down side in this strategy is that it can land on the one dozen wager that the player didn’t bet, meaning they lose both bets.

Corners and double
One roulette strategy that works if you’re looking to maximize your wagers in a short amount of time is playing the corners on the inside, which allows you to bet four numbers at once at an 8:1 payout. On top of playing corners, a players starts with a set wager – for this example $20 – and with each win they double that amount. 

So if the initial $20 corner bet comes through at 8:1 odds, paying $160, they will make a wager of $40 on the corners in the next game. If that bet hits, paying out $320, the next game with have an $80 wager on the board. Even if that $80 bet loses, the player is up $400 over the previous two games. They would then start back at the set amount, which in this example was $20.
