How to play and win at Casino War



  Tweet

Here’s how to play Casino War at some of the best bitcoin casinos.


Casino War is a familiar game to most, as the original version is a popular household game and one of the first card games many people ever picked up. The casino version does differ from the original, which has been known to take hours to complete, the biggest difference being that you bet on each hand. Here’s a quick breakdown of how to play Casino War and best strategies to use at some of the best bitcoin casinos.

The deal
Each play antes up with whatever sized bet they want to place and is dealt a single card. The dealer is also dealt one card and the game begins. Cards are ranked in standard format with deuces being the lowest card in the deck, face cards ranked Jack, Queen, King and Ace as the highest-valued card in the game. Some online and brick-and-mortar casinos offer a “tie bonus” which can be wagered and pays out at 10/1 odds if the player and dealer end up with the same ranked hand in that particular game.

Paying out and War
After the dealer is dealt his card, he grades each player’s card. Those that have a higher ranked card than the dealer are paid out even money on their wager while those with a lower ranked card loss their original bet.

In the case of a player and dealer having the same ranked card – such as both turning up a Jack – the player has two options. They can 1. Surrender and lose just half of their original bet or 2. Go to war.

Going to war entails the player placing another bet the same size as the original, and the house matching, and both the dealer and the player drawing another card. If the player’s second card is graded higher than the dealer, they win the war. If not, they lose. In the case that the player and dealer again draw the same ranked card, the player is awarded a special bonus payout.

Tips and strategy
You may be best to avoid betting the “tie bonus” altogether. While the 10/1 payout is juicy and tempting, the house edge on this bet is a massive 18.5 percent – one of the worst wagers a player can make on the entire casino floor. Seeing that Casino War already has near 3 percent house edge to begin with – compared to blackjack which can be as low as 0.28 percent – don’t throw yourself to the wolves if you can help it.

Another strategy point is to never surrender when a tie occurs with the dealer. Rather than automatically forfeit half your original wager – a loss no matter how you look at it – you have a shot at winning twice your wager when you decide to go to war. A chance to double up or a sure loss? It’s not too hard to figure out which move the best one.
