Sic Bo: What is it and how do I win?



  Tweet

Here’s a quick look at how to bet the Sic Bo board at a bitcoin casino.


If you’re a fan of craps or roulette – and really, who isn’t? – then chances are you will love Sic Bo. This game originated in China and uses dice and various bets placed on a board, which shares similarities with those popular casino games mentioned above. However, instead of the player shooting two dice, the dealer rolls three. That’s just one of the ways Sic Bo varies from modern craps and roulette. Here’s a quick look at how to bet the Sic Bo board at a bitcoin casino:

Top of the board
Like the craps and roulette tables, there are numerous spots on the board representing different bets and payouts. At the top of the Sic Bo board are three main wagers. At the corners, players will find the “Small” and “Big” bets – paying Even money - which allow the player to bet on whether the total of the three dice will be between 4-10 or if it will be 11-17. Next to those, you’ll find the double bets, which allow players to bet a specific number will come up twice, paying 8/1 odds. And in the middle of the top row, players can wager on whether three of a kind will come up – either betting a specific number to show three times (150/1) or just wagering on three of a kind (24/1).

Specific total
Below the top line are spots for each individual roll total from the three dice, listed 4-17 with differing payouts on each total (a total roll of three or 16 would pay for triples). Four and 17 come with the biggest payouts at 50/1 because there is only one combination of three dice that will produce those totals, while a total of five or 16 pays 18/1. Here’s how the remaining exact total bets pay: 6 and 15 (14/1), 7 and 14 (12/1), 8 and 13 (8/1), 9 and 12 (6/1), 10 and 11 (5/1).

Combination and single bets
Players can wager on a combination of any two numbers to show up in the roll, for example a one and six or a combo of three and five. There are 15 different combination bets players can make in Sic Bo, all of which pay 5/1 odds.

Below the combination bets are the single bet spots, which allow players to place a wager on a number that they think will show up in one of the three dice rolled. Obviously, these spots are numbered 1-6. Players will win Even money if their selected number comes up, but can win 2/1 if it shows up twice, and 3/1 if all three dices end up on the wagered number.
