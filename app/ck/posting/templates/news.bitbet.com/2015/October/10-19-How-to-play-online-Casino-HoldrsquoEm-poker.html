Here’s how to play Casino Hold’Em and hopefully win some free bitcoin


Texas Hold’Em has become the most popular variation of poker in the past two decades, thanks to the World Series of Poker and the rise in TV programming around the game. Most casinos have a high-limit poker rooms, where players can test their Hold’Em skill against others, but another version of the game is quickly gaining a following with online casino players.

Casino Hold’Em is a player-versus-dealer variation, which makes it much more convenient than trying to round up enough players for a classic game of Texas Hold’Em. It also has a much quicker pace, which appeals to many poker players. Here’s a breakdown of how to play Casino Hold’Em and hopefully win some free bitcoin:

Getting started
The game is played with a standard 52-card deck and as mentioned, players go against the dealer – not each other. Each player antes up to start the game and are dealt two cards, the same as Texas Hold’Em, along with the dealer. And, just like Texas Hold’Em, three cards are dealt face up, also known as the flop. It’s from here that players can choose whether to continue with the game or fold.

Calling
If the player doesn't wish to continue with the hand, they fold and forfeit their original ante bet. But if the player wants to continue with the hand, they must call – or make another ante bet equal to their original wager. So a player who placed an original ante of $20 must make another bet of $20 to continue with the game.

Final two cards
Once all players are settled, the dealer places two more cards face up, to make a total of five showing on the table. Players and the dealer then make their highest ranked poker hands from their two original hole cards and the five cards showing on the table. Most Casino Hold’Em games have a required qualifying hand for the dealer to have in order for there to be action. Most casinos set that qualifying hand as a pair of fours or higher.

Grading and payouts
If the dealer does not have a qualifying hand, most often the original ante bet pays at 1:1 and the call bet is returned. However, casinos offer an AnteWin pay scale which rewards higher-ranked hands, putting a Flush at 2:1, Full House at 3:1, Four of a Kind at 10:1, Straight Flush 20:1, and Royal Flush at 100:1 pay outs. This payout scale also applies when the dealer does have a qualifying hand. If the player wins, the original ante is graded according to the pay scale and the call bet wins at 1:1. If the dealer’s hand is better than the player’s, all bets are lost.
