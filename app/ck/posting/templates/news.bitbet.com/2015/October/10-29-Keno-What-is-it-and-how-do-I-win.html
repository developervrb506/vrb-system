Keno: What is it and how do I win?

In the casino world – both online and brick-and-mortar – Keno is a staple that many players have no clue about. It sits next to games like Bingo, which carries a stigma as an old lady’s game. But let us tell you, Keno is not just for the “blue hairs”. Here’s a basic introduction to Keno so you can get started playing at the best bitcoin website:

Balls, balls, balls

Keno is a lottery-style game that may conjure up memories of watching the nightly lotto numbers bounce out of a machine on the local news. There are 80 balls inside the “bubble” numbered No. 1 through No. 80, and 20 of which are drawn at random.

Betting
Most often, players can wager on up to 20 numbers at once and depending on how many of those hit – and their original wager – winnings are calculated according to the casinos payout table. Players can even play a “catch” in which they pick 20 numbers not to hit.

Some casinos offer better winnings on players who hit more numbers with fewer selected, such as hitting four of six selections compared to hitting four of 15 selections. However, the more numbers you play the more you need to get correct in order to qualify for a payout. Betting four numbers will payout if you hit two or more, but betting 10 numbers requires you to correctly hit at least five to qualify for a win.

Strategy
As with any game involving random draws, like Keno, there’s no surefire strategy that will consistently improve your odds of winning, like say in game such as blackjack. However, there are some tips to consider when picking your numbers and making your bets.

Keno players are suggested to play between four and eight numbers each game, which mathematically gives you the best odds of paying out. Taking fewer or more numbers than that range will impact your winning percentage and your winning probability.

Another strategy is wagering with the Martingale System, which is a fancy way of saying you should chase your losses. If you wager a set amount of numbers every game and lose on an original wager of $10, then you would bet $20 the next game to make up for that loss.

If you were to lose that second game, you would bet $40 on the third game. Once the player wins, the bet is reduced to the original set wager – in this case $10. This is a risky strategy because a cold streak can do a lot of damage quickly.
