Keno
Keno is a great game, tons of fun, and perfect for those players looking for some easy betting action. But you don’t want to take Keno too seriously. This game has a house advantage of about 25 percent, which is massive compared to games like blackjack which have a house edge of just 0.28 percent. The reason Keno has such a big house lean is that it’s basically a lottery, paying out if you pick some of the 20 drawn balls from a total of 80.

Seven in craps
Making a bet on a single roll isn’t the wisest prospect in the game of craps, especially since there are so many other options on the table. There are six ways to roll seven, making it a 1/6 shot every time the dice are rolled. “Big Red” as it’s called, because saying “Seven” is bad luck at the craps table, will pay out at 4/1 odds and holds a hulking house advantage of almost 17 percent – doesn’t seem worth it in the end. You’re better off playing the “Pass” or “No Pass” odds, which can still involve the shooter rolling a seven but come with a much lower house edge, at 1.41 and 1.36 percent respectively.

Tie bet in baccarat
Each baccarat coup has three possible outcomes that players can wager on: player, banker and tie. You wager on which one will have the higher hand, with “player” paying out at Even money and “banker” coming with a 5 percent commission. However, the tie often pays out at 8/1 odds but rarely happens, at just 9.543 percent. That gives a tie bet a house advantage of more than 14 percent. Players are better off focusing on the “player” and “banker” bets, which have a house edge of 1.24 and 1.06 percent.
