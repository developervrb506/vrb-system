A beginner’s guide to playing Pontoon



  Tweet

Pontoon is the British version of 21 and closely resembles Spanish blackjack.


The name pontoon conjures up visions of shirtless bros drinking beer and BBQing on the deck of a funny looking boat. However, the card game of the same name is quickly becoming a popular variation of blackjack. Found at most brick-and-mortar spots and online casinos, pontoon is the British version of 21 and closely resembles Spanish blackjack. Here’ a beginner’s guide to the game to help you when you are bitcoin gambling:

Dealing and values
Like regular blackjack, each player is given two cards each but in Pontoon the dealer is only dealt one and then receives a second later in the game. And, like blackjack, the objective is to beat the dealer’s hand by getting closer to 21 without going over. Cards 2 through 9 hold their numerical values but all 10 cards are discarded from the deck and face cards take on a value of 10, while Aces can be either one or 11.

Surrender
Unlike regular blackjack, players are offered a chance to surrender before the dealer receives their second card. If the dealer is showing an ace or face card, a player may choose to surrender and receive half of their original wager back. However, it the dealer gets a 21 – Ace and face card – they do not receive anything back. If a player does not surrender, they continue on – choosing to hit or stand – until they settle on a hand or go bust.

Pairs
Players can make a side wager before each hand that pays out if they are dealt a pair. If they are dealt two cards of the same value, they have the option to split the hand into two and must put down an additional wager that is the same value as the first.

Playing the dealer
Once all remaining players have their hands settled, the dealer finally draws their additional cards and tries to beat the players’ hands. Most casinos will have the dealer hit on a soft 17, which is an Ace and 6. In pontoon, a player can have a card count of 21 and still beat a dealer that draws a natural 21 – Ace and face card – which is not something you find in other forms of blackjack.
