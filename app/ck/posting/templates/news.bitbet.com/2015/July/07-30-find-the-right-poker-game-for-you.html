Slower pace: Pai Gow/Tri-Card poker
If you’re just starting to play poker and don’t want to get too far ahead of yourself or if you just enjoy a slower gambling experience, Pai Gow and Tri-Card Poker are two of the better options. Due to the number of pushes against the dealer in both games, the rate of play can be quite methodical.

Pai Gow offers players a chance to play two poker hands out of seven cards against the dealer’s best two poker hands, often times winning one and losing another. Tri-Card poker offers many bonuses, including the Pair Plus which can win or lose regardless of how the actual hand is played.

Cerebral game: Texas Hold’Em
If you’re not a fan of “the luck of the draw” and want to have a little more control over how your poker game plays out, than Texas Hold’Em is your game. Players are dealt two cards and wager accordingly as five hidden cards are revealed. The first is a series of three cards called the flop, then a fourth named the turn, and finally a fifth called the river.

Players compete against each other and can use betting patterns to their advantage. Some Hold’Em players will “slow play” a good hand, as to not scare off others and lure in more bets while some may be more aggressive betting with a weaker hand in order to make it seem like their two-card hole is much stronger than it really is.

Fast paced: Video poker
If you’re looking to pick up the pace or just trying to kill some time, video poker offers everything you love about the game but with quick hands and plenty of big bonuses - which you can instantly withdraw with BitBet. There are a number of video poker variations, including Jokers Wild, Jacks or Better, Aces and Faces, and Deuces Wild.

Rather than playing against a dealer or other players, video poker has a payout system that rewards the rarer poker hands with larger winnings. A Royal Flush will payout more than a straight which ranks higher on the pay scale than a pair.
