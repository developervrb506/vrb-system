Kentucky Derby Live Long Shots



  Tweet

Kentucky Derby has been dominated by three horses – American Pharoah, Carpe Diem, and Dortmund, but these live long shots may do the trick


The Kentucky Derby is the one time of the year when it seems like everyone has some horse racing bets down. The lead up to the Run for the Roses has been dominated by three horses – American Pharoah, Carpe Diem, and Dortmund – but if you’re looking to strike it big in the first jewel of horse racing’s Triple Crown, these live long shots may do the trick.

Mubtaahij
This horse punched its ticket to Churchill Downs with a textbook run in the $2 million UAE Derby in late March. Mubtaahij was just off the lead heading into the turn and then exploded for a victory by eight lengths. He has won at 1 3/16 miles, so the Kentucky Derby distance isn’t an issue and he’s peaked since being switched over to dirt tracks.

Materiality
He has the blood line to compete in the Triple Crown events, sired by Afleet Alex who won the Preakness and Belmont in 2005. Materiality is one of the more inexperienced runners in the Kentucky Derby field but has shown plenty of promise in the Derby prep schedule, including an underdog win in the Florida Derby. However, how this horse will react to the big stage of Derby Day is an unknown and makes him a wild card.

International Star
He took the Louisiana Derby and won the Fair Grounds' LeComte and Risen Star stakes heading into the Kentucky Derby but a lot of International Star’s chances are tightly connected to his draw. This horse and jockey Miguel Mena love to slip to the inside and ride the rail, so if his starting gate allows him to get to the inside he could present some added value. If not, he could struggle to find his comfort zone.

Firing Line
This horse won the Sunland Derby in March with a record-breaking run, winning by 14 1/4 lengths and setting the 1 1/8-mile track record at 1:47.39. Firing Line went toe-to-toe with Kentucky Derby favorite Dortmund as both a two and three year old, finishing runner-up in each race. He has the ability to track the pace and still have energy left for a strong finish.
