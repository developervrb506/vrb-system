Three Simple Ways to Become a Better Blackjack Player



  Tweet

Three basic casino blackjack tips can sharpen your game and instantly make you a smarter and more profitable player


It doesn’t matter if you’re just starting out or if you’ve been playing blackjack for years, these three basic casino blackjack tips can sharpen your game and instantly make you a smarter and more profitable player.

Go ahead, count cards
There are a few myths out there concerning counting cards that scare people off this practice. One: Counting cards is illegal. Well, that one doesn’t fly if you’re playing online poker with no dealer to pick up the scent. But even in brick-and-mortar casinos with live dealers, counting cards is an everyday thing for blackjack players and it’s not going to get you tossed from the table or worse.

Two: Counting cards is hard. Nope. There are numerous methods to counting cards and predicting what is coming out of the shoe next, but among the simplest is the Hi-Lo method which can be taught and perfected in little time.

Stand and Hit
Understanding how the game works in your favor is key when chooses to stand or hit on a certain combination of cards. Always hit on a soft 17 – with an ace – knowing that most dealers must stay on 17 and you have an insurance policy with that ace should a card bigger than a four come up. In the same vein, you should always hit on 12 through 16 if the dealer is showing a seven or higher. They only need to pull a 10 or ace to beat you.

Splits and double downs
Splitting cards and doubling down on advantageous hands is a quick way to build your chip stack, but only if it’s done correctly. Splitting aces is wise, doubling your chances of blackjack, while splitting fives is considered a poor choice, taking away a 10 and a shot at a solid hand.

Players are encouraged to double down on 10 and 11, with a good shot at a 20 or 21 coming out of the shoe. On the flip side, you should be cautious of a double down if you are holding a 10 or 11 and the dealer is showing a 9 or 10. It could make for some heartbreaking – and costly – losses. Many players will be aggressive and double down on their hand when a dealer is showing a 2 through 6, which are considered bust cards.
