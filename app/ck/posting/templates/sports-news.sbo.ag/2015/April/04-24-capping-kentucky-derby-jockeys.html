Capping Kentucky Derby Jockeys



  Tweet

Here’s a look at the top jockeys and their mounts as we close in on the May 2 Run for the Roses


A lot of attention is paid to the horses running in the Kentucky Derby, and rightfully so. However, there is online horse wagering value in looking at the little guys driving those thoroughbreds toward the finish line at Churchill Downs.

An experienced jockey knows how to read a race, best pace and place his horse, while also keeping them calm and focused in the biggest race of the year.

Here’s a look at the top jockeys and their mounts as we close in on the May 2 Run for the Roses.

Victor Espinoza (American Pharoah +275):
Espinoza is riding the Kentucky Derby favorite and looking for his third title win at Churchill Downs, winning the Derby aboard War Emblem in 2002 and California Chrome last year. He’ll get a good feel for the track while riding Stellar Wind in the Kentucky Oaks Friday. Espinoza got a lot of credit for helping California Chrome find his championship form and many see the same results with American Pharoah.

Mike Smith (Dortmund +450):
Smith is one of the most veteran riders in all of horse racing, having led thoroughbreds to championships since the 90’s. He took mount Giacomo to the Kentucky Derby winner’s circle as a 50/1 long shot in 2005, which is his only Derby title. However, he’s won the Preakness, Belmont Stakes and owns the most Breeder’s Cup wins in the history of the sport with 21 victories.

John Velazquez (Carpe Diem +500):
Velazquez is one of the most decorated and profitable jockeys in horse racing, earning over $300 million for his Hall of Fame career. He won the Kentucky Derby aboard Animal Kingdom after a series of strange pre-race events in 2011 and has a reputation for bringing his horses from the back of the back. He brings plenty of Kentucky Derby experience with this being his 17th Run for the Roses.

Christophe Soumillon (Mubtaahij +800):
This horse has been deemed the X-factor of the Kentucky Derby, sitting behind the top three favorites but showing the ability to run the distance and also drive from the pack. Soumillon doesn’t have the same name brand as some of his fellow jockeys, with the 33-year-old Belgian racing the majority of his events overseas, but he has the vote of confidence from trainer Mike de Kock.
