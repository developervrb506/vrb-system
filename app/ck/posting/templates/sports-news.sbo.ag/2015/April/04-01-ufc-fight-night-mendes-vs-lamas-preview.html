UFC Fight Night: Mendes vs. Lamas preview



  Tweet

The smallest fighters can pack the biggest punch, especially when sizing up the odds


The smallest fighters can pack the biggest punch, especially when sizing up the sportsbook online betting odds for this Saturday’s UFC Fight Night card. Some of the most promising lightweight and featherweight fighters in the sport are on display in Virginia.

Chad Mendes (+318) vs. Ricardo Lamas (-443)
The odds for this main event featherweight bout look a little one-sided, and a lot of that has to due with Mendes inability to get over the talent hump at 145 pounds. He’s been given a title shot twice during his tenure in the UFC, only to fall to champion Jose Aldo both times. Mendes clashes with another elite-level contender in Lamas, who seems on track to face Aldo for the championship crown.

Lamas also lost to Aldo in his first meeting with the featherweight title holder, but has since won two straight fights to put himself back in the championship conversation. His first-round submission of Dennis Bermudez was a statement victory in the division and a big reason why he carries a heavier price tag in this matchup.

Jorge Masvidal (-125) vs. Al Iaquinta (-105)
If the odds are any indication, this co-main event is shaping up to be one of the most exciting bouts on the card. Both 155-pounders love to stand are throw, so bettors should be wary of both fighters' KO power.

Masvidal is a terrific boxer, winning three straight via the judges’ cards, and throws the third-highest accuracy in the division. Iaquinta, who is more of a grappler looking for takedowns, has proven he has power with three straight victories via KO.

Michael Chiesa (-353) vs. Mitch Clarke (+253)
This bout present two very hungry fighters trying to make some noise in one of the most competitive divisions in the UFC. Chiesa has plenty of public appeal as the TUF 15 winner and his lone blemishes have come against stiff competition.

Clarke is a very dangerous submission fighter but isn’t as versatile standing up like Chiesa, who proved his toughness in his last fight with veteran Joe Lauzon. He lost via doctor’s stoppage after suffering a massive gash above his right eye.
