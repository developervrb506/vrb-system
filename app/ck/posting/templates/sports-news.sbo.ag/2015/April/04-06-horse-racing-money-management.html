Horse Racing Money Management

Handicapping horses and knowing what to look for in a winner is just part of what it takes to be successful at horse race betting. Being smart enough to properly manage your bankroll will help keep you in the black at the track.

Balancing your Bets
No matter the size of your wallet, horse bettors should limit their wagers from 1 to 5 percent of their overall bankroll. The size of the bet depends on how strongly you feel about a play. If you’ve got a good angle on a sure-fire winner and you’re betting with a bankroll of $5,000, then put a $250 wager – or 5 percent – down on that horse. If you’re not as confident in that pick, then trim the percentage down.

Dedicate your Bankroll
If you like to bet straight picks as well as horizontal bets or exotics, make sure you have a set amount of your bankroll dedicated to just those types of wagers. If you’re dealing with a bankroll of $5,000, then split $2,500 on straight winner bets and the other $2,500 on horizontal options like trifecta, superfectas, and Pick 6s.

It is also a solid practice to avoid some types of wagers based on your bankroll limits. If you’re dealing with a bankroll between $500 and $200, you will want to avoid some of those longer horizontal odds, like trifectas and superfectas, and limit your exotics to exactas, daily doubles and Pick 3s. If you bankroll is $100 or less, you’ll want to stick with just straight winner bets until you build it up and can afford to risk a little more on those exotic options.

Keep Track
Don’t just bundle all your bets into one category. Keep track of your wager types, size of bets, and odds available. Not only will you be able to easily weigh your wins and losses but you can see which wagers are working for you and which ones aren’t. You can use this record to make adjustments to your betting patterns and also indicate which aspects of horse race betting you need to improve on.
