NBA Playoffs Betting Preview



  Tweet

The NBA postseason tips off this weekend with eight great matchups Saturday and Sunday


The NBA postseason tips off this weekend with eight great matchups Saturday and Sunday.

Here’s those Round 1 series and their NBA betting lines in a glance:

EAST

Atlanta Hawks (-1,300) vs. Brooklyn Nets (+800)
The Hawks may have been the top team in the East but this franchise has yet to prove it has what it takes to win in the playoffs. Brooklyn punched its ticket to the playoffs with a 13-6 run but is there anything left in the tank.

Cleveland Cavaliers (-5,000) vs. Boston Celtics (+1,700)
LeBron James has never lost a first-round playoff series in his career but could there be betting value with the Celtics as big underdogs? Boston was the best underdog bet in the East, and took two wins over the Cavs at the end of the season.

Chicago Bulls (-700) vs. Milwaukee Bucks (+525)
The Bulls got healthy just in time for the playoffs, with Derrick Rose and Jimmy Butler back, but how will they hold up in the postseason grinder? Milwaukee could hold value in this first-round matchup, with a tough defense and deep bench.

Toronto Raptors (-190) vs. Washington Wizards (+161)
Toronto struggled in the second half of the schedule and the health of Kyle Lowry is a major concern in this series. The Wizards will test the Raptors backcourt depth with the dynamic duo of John Wall and Bradley Beal.

WEST

Golden State Warriors (-5,000) vs. New Orleans Pelicans (+1,700)
The Warriors’ scoring prowess should be enough to overwhelm New Orleans, playing at a much faster pace than their first round foe. However, Pelicans star Anthony Davis will exploit Golden State’s weakness in the paint and on the boards.

Houston Rockets (-280) vs. Dallas Mavericks (+230)
This series will at the very least be entertaining, with two of the top 3-point offenses in the league squaring off. But don’t blindly shoot for the Over when the Mavericks and Rockets meet up. They went Under in all four meetings this season.

Los Angeles Clippers (+143) vs. San Antonio Spurs (-173)
This is the only playoff series in which the lower seed is actually the favorite. And why not? We’re talking about the defending NBA champs, who finished the season on a 21-4 streak. The Clippers did win the last two meetings, though.

Memphis Grizzlies (-180) vs. Portland Trail Blazers (+155)
It’s the Grizzlies' dominant inside game against the Blazers' potent perimeter shooting. Memphis tops the league in points in the paint, boasting the deepest frontcourt in the NBA. The Blazers can stretch those big men with their touch from outside.
