MMA Silva Testing Positive For Steroids



  Tweet

UFC middleweight champion Anderson Silva, a top Mixed Martial Arts fighter will not be on top of anyone's list.


“I’ve been competing in this sport for a very long time. I have been thoroughly tested many times and have never had a positive drug test. I have not taken any performance enhancing drugs. My stance on drugs is, and will always be, the same. I’m an advocate for a clean sport.” No, those are not the words of Lance Armstrong, dredged up from old newspaper archives.

This time the speaker is UFC middleweight champion Anderson Silva who, if he isn't the greatest Mixed Martial Arts fighter of all time won't be far from the top of anyone's list. His Ruthian accomplishments in the sport include Most Successful Title Defenses (10), Most Consecutive Wins in the UFC (16), Most Knockdowns Landed in UFC History (17), and Longest UFC Title Reign (2457 days - about seven years, the same number of consecutive Tours de France won by the disgraced Armstrong). Now a shadow has been cast over that stellar record with the news that the Brazilian champion tested positive for anabolic steroids in a random out-of-competition drug test conducted on January 9.

So once again we turn to the sports pages and are introduced to names that sound like they were pulled out of a Russian novel, this time Drostanolone and Androstane. And we get to learn all about "A" samples and "B" samples. And enjoy the numbing explanations from lab technicians about how urine samples are handled that transport us back to high school chemistry class where no one wants to be. And of course we get the entertaining explanations from the athlete's camp how these results just cannot be so.

The timing could not be worse for the higher ups in charge of fueling the impressive growth of Mixed Martial Arts. Jon "Bones" Jones, who became a fan favorite with his flashy striking technique and wrestling prowess, tested positive for cocaine metabolites in early January. Even Silva's most recent opponent, Nick Diaz, was outed as a marijuana user.

Response to Silva's failed drug test has been the expected hodgepodge from "let's wait until we hear both sides in a full hearing" to "this is just more evidence that all the top Mixed Martial Arts fighters are juiced." Suspicion is heightened since Silva, nearing 40 years of age, opted to forego retirement after breaking both his left fibula and tibia in a fight against Chris Weidman in December 2013. Advanced age and rapid returns to form after injuries raise red flags among observers in every sport in the performance-enhancing drug era. The UFC also did itself no favors by permitting the Anderson-Diaz bout to go on after both fighters had failed drug tests. In Diaz's case there were several dirty tests leading up to the January 31 match but Nevada Athletic Commission executive Bob Bennett green-lighted the card after a clean sample was finally provided.

So two of Mixed Martial Arts' greatest stars have been tainted by testing positive for drugs. One of our newest sports is dealing with one of sport's oldest problems - athletes looking to get an edge with banned substances. Unfortunately there are plenty of playbooks from other sports for Mixed Martial Arts to study in recovering from this black eye.
