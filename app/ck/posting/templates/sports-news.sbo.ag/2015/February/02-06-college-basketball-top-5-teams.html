College Basketball Top 5 Teams



  Tweet

Kentucky Wildcats were anointed the Number One college basketball team in the 2014-15 season


The Kentucky Wildcats were anointed the Number One college basketball team in the 2014-15 season as soon as they stepped off the court in AT&T Stadium in Arlington, Texas as 60-54 losers to Connecticut in the national championship game. Nothing has changed since. There were a couple of unexpected extensions into overtime in early Southeastern Conference play but the undefeated Wildcats' performance thus far has pundits debating not whether they are the best basketball team in the country this year but of all time. As usual John Calipari's squad is stuffed with blue-chip McDonald's All-Americans, only one of whom is an upperclassman. He often substitutes them into the game en masse, hockey style.

But those who only see the gaudy undefeated record may not be aware that this Kentucky team is built on defense. The Wildcats give up only 81.7 points per 100 possessions, the stingiest defense the college game has ever seen since that metric was introduced in the early 2000s - by a whopping 3.4 points. So even if Kentucky's shots are not falling on a particular evening the opposition still has to contend with that historically stifling defense to defeat the 'Cats.

For much of the 2014 March Madness the Wisconsin Badgers were the best team in the tournament, until they lost by a point to Kentucky in the National Semifinals. Frank Kaminsky has kept his Badgers among the nation's elite, doing so in familiar fashion. Wisconsin's grinding style limits teams to 56 points a game. They are the class of the competitive Big Ten and while a plodding style of play may not win fans it certainly wins game.

You don't have to say that twice to Tony Bennett whose Virginia Cavaliers play to the second-slowest tempo in the nation. Ask Georgia Tech who walked off the floor of John Paul Jones Arena with 28 points in a January loss. The Cavaliers made it to 19-0 before stumbling at home to conference rival Duke. Both Virginia and Duke, led by the sublime Jahlil Okafor, are possibilities to receive Number One seeds at next month's big dance. Even while undefeated no one was comparing Virginia to Kentucky and for good reason - when Bennett looks down his bench he doesn't see a single McDonald's All-American, a rarity for an elite team.

The Kansas Jayhawks came into the 2014-15 season with high expectations which lasted precisely 41 minutes. After an opening tune-up in a win against the University of California at Santa Barbara the #5 Jayhawks were dismantled in a neutral site game by Kentucky 72-40. That loss dropped the Hawks down to the mid-teens in the national rankings but Kansas has clawed its way back, thanks in large part to a stellar 8-1 record in the Big 12, the toughest conference in the country top to bottom. Top-heavy conferences like the ACC with Virginia, Duke, Notre Dame, Louisville and North Carolina have no shortage of Final Four dreamers but there is a good chance for ten of the Big 12 teams to get invitations to the tournament.

Arizona has been the class of the PAC-12 for so long it is easy to forget that UCLA once ruled the college basketball universe. NBA players rotate through coach Sean Miller's program with such regularity that the tired trope of "reloading, not rebuilding" is entirely appropriate in Tucson. Arizona has been the victim of mystifying defeats in past seasons but they are once again in a position to win a national championship. As always "defense" is the watchword for Miller-coached teams which results in such beat-downs as the 57-34 hurting the Wildcats put on a 14-6 Oregon State squad at the end of January. It just may turn out that the 2014-15 season will end with a pair of Wildcat teams vying to see just who is indeed the more ferocious.
