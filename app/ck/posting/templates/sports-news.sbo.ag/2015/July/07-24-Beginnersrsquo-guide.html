However, online casinos offer Baccarat at various limits allowing everyone to enjoy this game. Here’s a beginners guide to playing Baccarat at sports gambling websites:

Player and Banker
There are two hands in the game of Baccarat, the Player and the Banker hands. The object of the game is to wager on which hand – Player or Banker – will have a total closest to nine. You can also wager that the hands will tie, which often comes with bigger payouts.

Card values
In Baccarat, cards 2 through 9 are at face value while Aces are used as one. Tens and face cards have a zero value. For example, a hand of a 2 and 5 has a value of seven while a hand of 3 and King has a value of three. Most Baccarat tables will pay automatically on a two-card hand of eight or nine, also known as a natural. The Player must stand on a hand of six or seven.

A third card must be drawn if the first two cards equal five or less, however, any card total over 10 has 10 subtracted from it, so a three-card draw of 4, Ace, and 8 (13) is equal to three. A three-card draw of King, 2, and 8 would have a value of zero, with face cards worth nothing and the 2 and 8 totaling 10 which has a value of zero.

Betting
In Baccarat, a wager on the Banker hand will cost you a little more. Most casinos have a 5 percent commission on the Banker hand due to the fact that its actions are dependent on the Player hand, which is dealt first.

Since the Player hand is completed first the Banker hand does not have to react if the Player hand finishes with a lower score. The Banker hand generally wins 50.68 percent of the time, with pushes going to the dealer.
