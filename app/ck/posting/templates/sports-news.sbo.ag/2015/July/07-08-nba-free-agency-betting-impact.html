NBA Free Agency Betting Impact



  Tweet

Plenty of big-name players swapped sides, forcing oddsmakers to adjust the NBA Championship futures odds.


July opened with a bang for NBA teams on the free agent hunt. Plenty of big-name players swapped sides, forcing oddsmakers to adjust the NBA Championship futures odds. Here’s a look at the biggest free agency signings and trades, and how they impact the betting market:

San Antonio Spurs
Questions surrounded the future of the franchise, with the Spurs’ trio of Tim Duncan, Tony Parker and Manu Ginobili getting older by the bucket. But San Antonio answered in a big way, not only hanging on to Kawahi Leonard and Danny Green but also adding power forward/center LaMarcus Aldridge and veteran David West, swinging power back to the Spurs in the Western Conference. San Antonio entered the offseason as big as 14/1 but is now down to 3/1.

Dallas Mavericks
Mark Cuban dug deep into his pockets to make an offseason splash, adding center DeAndre Jordan and swingman Wesley Matthews to the roster this summer. The Mavericks have a solid starting five with long-range bombers in Dirk Nowitzki, Chandler Parsons and Matthews. Dallas was around 40/1 to win the NBA title when 2015-16 futures opened, with so many contenders in the West. The Mavs are down to 25/1 to win it all.

Milwaukee Bucks
The Bucks broke through in 2014-15, making the playoff cut in the East despite a slew of injuries to their starting lineup. Milwaukee wasn’t content to rest on that improvement and added center Greg Monroe to an exciting group of young players like Giannis Antetokounmpo, Jabari Parker and Michael Carter-Williams. The Bucks were originally around 80/1 to win the title this coming season but are now priced at 50/1.

Los Angeles Lakers
The Lakers missed out on a marquee free agent – again – with Aldridge, Monroe and Kevin Love all passing over Los Angeles. The Lakers did wheel and deal for center Roy Hibbert, giving them a much sturdier frontcourt with the 7-footer and Julius Randle returning from injury. Los Angeles also added gunner Lou Williams and forward Brandon Bass. With Kobe Bryant back and stud draft pick De’Angelo Russell, L.A. hiccupped from 80/1 to 75/1.
