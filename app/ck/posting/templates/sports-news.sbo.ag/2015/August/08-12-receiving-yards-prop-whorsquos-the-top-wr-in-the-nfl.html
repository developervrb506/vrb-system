Most receiving yards prop: Who’s the top WR in the NFL?



  Tweet

Here’s a look at the favorites to top the league in receiving yards this upcoming season


The NFL is quickly becoming a receiver’s league. With the increase in passing and the hands-off policy the league has taken toward defending receivers, it’s no surprise the biggest stars in the NFL are the playmaking wideouts. Here’s a look at the favorites to top the league in receiving yards this upcoming season so you can make your football bets online:

Favorites:
Antonio Brown, Pittsburgh (+500), Dez Bryant, Dallas (+500), Julio Jones, Atlanta (+500), Calvin Johnson, Detroit (+500)

Brown topped the NFL in yardage with 1,698 yards through the air last season. Pittsburgh’s playbook will continue to air it out, and with RB Le’Veon Bell suspended for the first two games of the season, Brown could get extra looks downfield.
Jones was third in receiving yards with 1,593 in 2014 – despite missing two games. The Falcons have a new offensive coordinator in Kyle Shanahan, who reportedly has a crush on the dynamic WR.

Bryant proved himself a go-to threat in the red zone with 16 touchdowns in 2014, but with Dallas running more often his yardage took a step back. This season, the Cowboys have a mess at RB so Dez could be called upon to carry this offense.

Megatron is still the most gifted receiver in the NFL but defenses throw double and triple coverage at the Lions' No. 1 option.
He watched teammate Golden Tate have a career year at his expense but still reeled in 1,077 yards.

Notables:
Jordy Nelson, Green Bay (+600), Demaryius Thomas, Denver (+650), Odell Beckham Jr., New York (+650)

Nelson has the best QB in the league slinging it downfield, helping the speedy WR tally 1,519 yards receiving last year, fourth most in the NFL. He averaged 15.5 yards per reception on 98 catches but now the cat is out of the bag and defenses are wise to Nelson.
Thomas signed a massive deal this offseason but his fortunes depend on the arm of Peyton Manning. Denver’s dynamic duo took a step back in the second half of the season in 2014, and the Broncos will run more under new coach Gary Kubiak.

It’s incredible just how much fame Beckham gained with his improbable catch against Dallas on Sunday Night Football. The Giants' second-year man from LSU benefited from having Victor Cruz out last season but will have to share catches with his fellow wideout in 2015.
