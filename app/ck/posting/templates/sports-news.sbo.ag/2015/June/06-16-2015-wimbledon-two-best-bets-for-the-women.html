2015 Wimbledon: Two Best Bets For The Women



  Tweet

Here our two best bets for the women’s side at Wimbledon.


The story of Wimbledon on the women’s side will once again be: can anyone beat Serena Williams? She’s already won the French Open and Australian Open this season, and she’s still holding the U.S. Open crown. That means that a win at Wimbledon would give her the grandslam. That’s why most people offering their tennis betting tips see her as the best bet for the upcoming event in London. At any rate, here our two best bets for the women’s side at Wimbledon.

Serena Williams (+175)

Like Djokovic, Williams is the favorite and the #1 player in the world, but she has won the first two Grand Slams of the year and three in a row overall going back to last year's U.S. Open. At 33 years old, Williams isn't as dominant as she used to be and simply steamrolling over her opponents, but she still finds ways to win when she has to. Don't forget, she has won Wimbledon five times, most recently in 2012, and perhaps the only thing she hasn't done in her career is win all four Grand Slams in the same year, which is still on the table and would be a massive feat, especially at her age (for either gender, that would be an amazing feat). Right now, it is Serena Williams, and then everyone else on the womens' side.

Simona Halep (+1200)

Halep is one of the up-and-comers in the tennis world at 23, and she has been getting better and better over the last two years. Those who give out expert tennis betting tips know that her best performance in a Grand Slam so far is making it to the final of the French Open last year, which she followed by getting to the semfinal at Wimbledon right after that tournament. She reached the quarterfinal for the second year in a row at the Australian Open and even though she fell in the second round at Roland Garros, that might have been the best thing for her. Halep is risen to third in the world (which shows you how large the gap is between Williams and everyone else), and with those odds, she is worth a shot.
