NBA early-bird ATS winners



  Tweet

Here the NBA teams getting off to a quick start covering the spread for sportsbook online betting.


The 2015-16 NBA schedule still has that new sneaker smell but it’s never too soon to start looking at which teams will provide profits this basketball season. Here are three NBA teams getting off to a quick start when it comes to covering the spread for people who do sportsbook online betting:

Toronto Raptors (3-0 SU, 3-0 ATS)
The Raptors made giant leaps last year and appear to be improving once again, with a 3-0 start to this season. Toronto defeated Eastern foes Indiana, Boston and Milwaukee by an average margin of 12 points, getting good production from big offseason acquisition DeMarre Carroll and some solid contributions off the bench. The Raptors face a much tougher task this week, traveling out West to play Dallas and Oklahoma City before stops in Orlando and Miami. Toronto will likely be an underdog in three of those four games, so there could still be ATS value with the Dinos.

Detroit Pistons (3-0 SU, 3-0 ATS)
The big stunner of the first week of the NBA season is the play of the Pistons. Detroit took it to Eastern rivals Chicago and Atlanta and knocked off Utah at home as well – all as an underdog. Defense looks to be the name of the game in Motown, where the Pistons have given up only 91.7 points per game to start the schedule. Following a home stand against the Pacers, Detroit hits the highway for a Western Conference tour, playing at Phoenix, Portland, Golden State, Sacramento, and the two L.A. teams. NBA bettors will soon find out if the Pistons are for real.

Golden State Warriors (3-0 SU, 3-0 ATS)
The defending NBA champs picked right back up where they left off, covering in their first three games after posting a 47-34-1 ATS record last year. Heading into Monday’s showdown with the Memphis Grizzlies, the Warriors have rolled over the early competition, picking up two wins over New Orleans – which they swept in the opening round of the playoffs last season – and dropping Houston by 20 points in the Toyota Center. If Golden State continues to cover at this rate, those spreads will likely get more inflated with every game. The Warriors will be sizable favorites playing Western bottom feeders like Denver, L.A. Lakers and Sacramento, then hosting Detroit in the next four games.
