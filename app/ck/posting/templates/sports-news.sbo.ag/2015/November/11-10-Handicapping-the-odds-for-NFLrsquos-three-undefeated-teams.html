Handicapping the odds for NFL’s three undefeated teams



  Tweet

Here’s how the oddsmakers at sport betting sites are treating their Super Bowl 50 futures


Week 9 of the NFL erased another team from the ranks of the undefeated, with the Denver Broncos falling on the road at Indianapolis. That leaves us with just three teams at 8-0. Here’s a closer look at what’s coming for these perfect squads and how the oddsmakers at sport betting sites are treating their Super Bowl 50 futures:

New England Patriots (8-0 SU, 5-2-1 ATS)
The Patriots’ “Deflategate” revenge tour is smashing through the completion and covering spreads right and left, which has many comparing the 2015 team to the 16-0 2007 New England squad. That team was a perfect 8-0 SU and ATS before failing to cover in Week 9 of that season.

This year, New England isn’t quite facing the same amount of chalk or covering at the same rate, but the Super Bowl odds seem to be comparable with Tom Brady & Co. priced as an overwhelming +220 faves to repeat as NFL champions. The Patriots do have a tougher second-half slate, with five of their final eight games away from home. New England is 2-1 ATS away from Gillette Stadium heading into a road date with the Giants in Week 10.

Cincinnati Bengals (8-0 SU, 7-0-1 ATS)
The Bengals have maintained their betting value despite the unblemished record. And while the spreads have been a touch inflated thanks to that success, Cincinnati doesn’t carry the same stigma as New England, so that value is hanging around. The Bengals are still 12/1 to win the Super Bowl – mainly because books know they’ll have to go through the Patriots to get there.

Cincinnati is at home giving 10.5 points to Houston this Monday but has some tougher tests ahead on the calendar. The Bengals play Arizona and St. Louis in back-to-back weeks following the Texans, and still have three more divisional games and a showdown at Denver to go. The only soft spots seem to be Houston in Week 10 and at San Francisco in Week 15.

Carolina Panthers (8-0 SU, 6-2 ATS)
Books are buying into Carolina a little more after knocking off Seattle, Philadelphia, Indianapolis, and Green Bay in its last four games. The Panthers are still 10/1 to win the Super Bowl, behind NFC foes Green Bay (+850) and Arizona (+900), however. Carolina has a road-heavy schedule in the second half of the season, playing five of its final eight games away from home.

The Panthers are just 4-point road chalk in Tennessee Sunday and have upcoming trips to Dallas, New Orleans, New York and Atlanta. The three home games come against the Redskins, Falcons and Buccaneers. The remarkable thing about this Panthers team is that outside of a 10-point spread versus the Saints, they’ve never been bigger than a 5-point favorite. The market is not buying into the Panthers’ success, so maybe it’s time you do.
