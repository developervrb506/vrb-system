2015 AFC Championship: Indianapolis Colts @ New England Patriots

The Patriots have the edge in Foxborough, but it could be closer than the spread

> Indianapolis Colts @ New England Patriots – 6:40 pm et

The NFL didn’t necessarily get the matchup they wanted for the AFC title game (Manning vs. Brady), but they did get a marquee quarterback battle in Tom Brady (New England Patriots) versus Andrew Luck (Indianapolis Colts). Luck (4,601 yards, 38 TDs) had better overall regular-season numbers than both Manning (4,454, 39 TDs) and Brady (4,029, 33 TDs), but is still very much the heir apparent to Brady’s throne.

Luck is the only QB of the four remaining teams who has never made it to a Super Bowl (let alone won it like Russell Wilson, Aaron Rodgers and Brady), but he is playing as well as his peers in just his third year in the league. The Colts offense is clicking at the right time and the defense stepped up in a major way in playoff wins over Cincinnati (26-10) and surprisingly on the road against Denver (24-13).

Tom Brady’s quest for a fourth Super Bowl ring trumps Luck’s ascendancy. Brady has a potent offense, especially in the passing attack with Rob Gronkowski (1,124 receiving yards, 12 TDs), Julian Edelman (92 catches, 972 yards) and Brandon LaFell (70 catches, 883 yards) but the Pats are vulnerable and beatable on their rare off-day.

New England doesn’t have a strong rushing attack, but neither do the Colts. LeGarrette Blount is the Pats’ lead back, with a platoon of Shane Vereen, Stevan Ridley and Brandon Bolden being little more than screen decoys. The Colts counter with Dan “Boom” Herron and maybe Ahmad Bradshaw or rookie Zurlon Tipton. Rushing yards will be at a premium with this beastly bunch.

Luck has the luxury of T.Y. Hilton (82 catches, 1,345 receiving yards), Reggie Wayne (64 catches, 779 receiving yards) and tight end Coby Fleener (774 receiving yards, 8 TDs) at his disposal. But the NE defense may have a bit more pedigree than their Indy counterparts. Darrelle Revis is still a shutdown corner and LB Jamie Collins (116 tackles, four forced fumbles) is coming along like gangbusters.

The Colts have a solid secondary led by Mike Adams (87 tackles, 5 INTs) and Vontae Davis (4 INTs) and some stellar linebackers in D’Qwell Jackson (140 tackles, 4 sacks) and Jerrell Freeman (94 tackles). The Pats have a couple of cold killers in the secondary in Devin McCourty (68 tackles, 2 INTs) and Patrick Chung (85 tackles).

Bill Belichick is the master-at-arms among coaches in this Conference champion pool, with three Super Bowl wins in five tries. The Colts’ Chuck Pagano is the only one of the four to have never been to the event as a head coach. It’s a good coaching matchup, as Pagano’s DC Greg Manusky proved himself a capable game-planner in his surprising stifling of Peyton Manning and the Broncos.

The Patriots have the edge in Foxborough, but it could be closer than the spread. Luck is a gunslinger capable of making this a game down to the wire. If the Colts defense holds stern again, it may take some late-game Brady heroics to send the Patriots to Glendale for Super Bowl XLIX.
