Tom Brady vs. Russell Wilson: Contrasting Styles, Winning Results



  Tweet

Sunday’s game features the first head-to-head Super-Bowl winning quarterback battle since 2011


Now that Super Bowl week is finally upon us, let’s hope the focus shifts away from deflated game balls and back squarely on this year’s two best teams. Bill Belichick can’t hide from the kerfuffle of course, this is not the first time a team he’s coached has been accused of attempting to gain a competitive advantage. The Spygate controversy caused a ruckus in 2007 as the Patriots were accused of systematically videotaping the opposition’s offensive and defensive signals. This tactic by the Patriots was thought to have impacted the outcome of New England’s victory over the Philadelphia Eagles in Super Bowl in XXXIX. Nothing was ever proven there.

So the Patriots enter the game as slight favorites amid a somewhat laughable swirling controversy. Tom Brady has more distractions in this, his sixth Super Bowl (a record for quarterbacks), than in previous years, but he certainly didn’t do himself any favors by attempting to address Deflategate with the media.

Brady’s offense was in top gear against the Colts, no amount of air in the ball could have helped Indianapolis get to the Super Bowl. With so many potent targets to choose from—Julian Edelman (17 catches, 172 yards in the postseason), TE Rob Gronkowski (10 catches, 136 yards, 2 TDs in the postseason), Danny Amendola (9 catches, 89 yards, 2 TDS in postseason), Brandon LaFell (74 catches, 953 yards, regular season) and RB Shane Vereen (52 catches, 447 yards, regular season)—Brady again has an embarrassment of riches at his disposal.

The Pats’ rushing attack—thought to be the team’s Achilles’ heel—has been bolstered by the play of LeGarrette Blount who has 149 yards on 33 carries and three touchdowns through two postseason games. Seattle’s hallmark is defense, but unlike last year the Legion of Boom is a little banged up. Two members of their shutdown secondary—Earl Thomas and Richard Sherman—have notable knocks. Sherman is dealing with a painful elbow injury he sustained against the Packers, he will play but clearly will not be 100 percent. Thomas is listed as questionable for Sunday’s game in Glendale as the safety is dealing with a shoulder injury also sustained against The Pack.

Seahawks strong safety Kam Chancellor is in top form with 16 tackles and an interception return for a TD in this year’s postseason. The linebacking corps of Bobby Wagner (19 tackles in this year’s postseason), Bruce Irvin (4 tackles, 1 sack) and K.J. Wright (12 tackles, 1 pass deflection in 2015 postseason) is looking stout and the defensive line continues to get pressure on the quarterback and shut down the run. Cliff Avril (2 sacks, 2015 postseason) is beasting upfront alongside Michael Bennett (7 tackles).

On offense, Seattle continues to get the most in crunch time out of Russell Wilson, RB Marshawn Lynch (216 postseason rushing yards) and their underrated receiving corps led by Doug Baldwin (144 receiving yards, 1 TD), Jermaine Kearse (164 receiving yards, 2 TDs) and TE Luke Willson (79 yards, 1 TD). Wilson had a horrific game through three-and-a-half quarters against Green Bay, throwing four interceptions and getting sacked five times; Wilson threw only seven interceptions all regular season. Wilson came alive when the odds were stacked against Seattle, helping the team score 15 late-game points in regulation and threw a dart to Kearse for the winning TD in overtime against Green Bay.

New England’s defense doesn’t get a lot of credit, but they arguably have the second-best defensive back in the NFL (behind Sherman) in Darrelle Revis. They also have an emerging talent in OLB Jamie Collins (15 tackles, 4 pass deflections, 1 INT) and experience on the D-Line in Vince Wilfork and Rob Ninkovich.

This Super Bowl is a dream matchup for the league as the defending-champ Seahawks face Brady who is desperately seeking his fourth Super Bowl ring. The outcome on paper here is basically a flip of the coin, but last year’s game was thought to be a win for Peyton Manning and the Broncos, on paper. Super Bowls come down to who can execute amid all the distractions of the most-hyped event on the sporting calendar. The mojo is strong with both teams but the Pats will find a way to win here, whether it’s within the rules of the game or not.
