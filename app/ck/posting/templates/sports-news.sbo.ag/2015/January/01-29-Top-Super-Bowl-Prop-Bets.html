A Look at the Top Super Bowl Prop Bets



  Tweet

Find out about the strangest prop bets you can bet on February 1


With Super Bowl XLIX a little more than a week away, there is a lot of discussion regarding the big game between the New England Patriots and Seattle Seahawks. While a lot of the talk is about the Patriots and the “deflate-gate” scandal, a lot of the other discussions have beens about the countless Super Bowl prop bets football fan can take part in. What are some of the strangest prop bets you can bet on February 1?

What Gatorade Color will Winning Coach Get Dumped On?
If trying to decide what bet to put your money on, there are enough choices, but one of the toughest could be what color of Gatorade (or any liquid) will be dumped on the winning coach. Currently, orange is the odds on favorite at 3/2, but not too far behind is the incredibly popular yellow color at 5/2. Looking for a long shot? How about green with 10/1 odds?

Let’s Get Political
One of the most sought after interviews around major sporting events each year is with President Barack Obama. He has chosen winners as often as losers when questioned and one prop bet wants to test who the American people think he will pick. Do you think he will pick the Seattle Seahawks (-140) to win it all, or do you think he will go with those a little closer to home, the New England Patriots (Even).

Another political-themed prop bet being thrown around is what will be higher, Russell Wilson’s passing yards (-15 ½) or the United States national average gas price (+15 ½) the day after the Super Bowl?

Multi-Sport Challenge
Prop bets don’t only look at what happens on Super Bowl Sunday, it also allows betting results from the big game against other popular sports. Julian Edelman averaged 69 yards per game during the regular season. In their two postseason games, he has averaged 86 yards per game receiving. This prop bet is titled, “What will be higher on games played Feb. 2?” Do you think Edelman will have more receiving yards at the Super Bowl (-150), or will Tiger Woods have a higher score on day 1 of the Masters (+120)? Or do you think that Carmelo Anthony will have more points than the Patriots will score in Glendale, AR?

It’s Not Just the Players
Of course, the Super Bowl is an entertainment spectacle which is why Idina Menzel will sing the National Anthem and Katy Perry will perform at the halftime show. They aren’t only entertaining fans, they are also entertaining prop bets for their performances. Some popular Menzel bets include the over/under of 2 minutes, 5 seconds, for her rendition of the United States National Anthem, and whether or not she will forget, or leave out, a word from the song. If you think she will forget a word, you’re looking at +400 odds, while you’re looking at -600 for the contrary.

For the pop princess Perry, the best prop bet you will find involves what she will be wearing when she comes out. A skirt or dress (4/5), pants below the knees (2/1), or shorts above the knees (2/1) are your choices, but all bets are out the window if she comes out a la Lady Gaga in a weird pod.
