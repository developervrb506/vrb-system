Ring in the New Year with these must-bet bowl games



  Tweet

Here’s a look at three of those matchups and their odds


Nothing sets the tone for a brand new year like battling through that New Year’s Eve hangover and betting the handful of marquee college football bowl games on January 1. While the College Football Playoff semifinals steal the spotlight on Dec. 31, the New Year’s Day bowls will not disappoint. Here’s a look at three of those matchups and their odds:

Outback Bowl: Northwestern Wildcats vs. Tennessee Volunteers (-8, 48)
Momentum is the name of the game for this New Year’s Day bowl – more specifically which team can maintain it. Both Northwestern and Tennessee closed the season on respective five-game winning streaks. The Wildcats leaned on their defense to build that streak, giving up an average of just under 17 points per game in that span. The Volunteers erupted for scores of 52 and 53 points during their winning run, however, the low total indicates a defensive battle between to run-heavy playbooks.

Fiesta Bowl: Notre Dame Fighting Irish vs. Ohio State Buckeyes (-6.5)
The Buckeyes aren’t where they’d like to be, having missed the cut for the playoff and settling for the Irish on Jan. 1. Ohio State coach Urban Meyer is a master motivator and a bowl betting legend at 9-2 SU and ATS for his career, but he may have his work cut out for him this time. Notre Dame returns top rusher C.J. Prosise for the Fiesta Bowl after missing the finale with an ankle injury. He’ll go up against an OSU defense that allowed the just 127.3 yards on the ground per game – 21st in the country.

Rose Bowl: Stanford Cardinal vs. Iowa Hawkeyes (+6, 53)
The Cardinal and Hawkeyes were so close to competing for the national title but settle for headlining the Rose Bowl – a New Year’s Day tradition. Many questioned the true strength of Iowa after playing a soft Big Ten schedule, however, the Hawkeyes took the fight to Michigan State in the conference championship, losing by only three points. Iowa has a stingy defense but probably not enough firepower to keep up with Stanford should this game turn into a shootout. With the total sitting around 53 points – the lowest total the Cardinal have seen since Week 8 – books could be finally giving the Hawkeyes some credit.
