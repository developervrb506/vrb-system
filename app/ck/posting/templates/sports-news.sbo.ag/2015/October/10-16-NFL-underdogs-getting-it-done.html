NFL underdogs getting it done



  Tweet

Here’s a look at three NFL teams that have been getting the job done.


In the world of football betting, it doesn’t matter if you win or lose on the scoreboard. All that matters is whether or not you covered – and these teams are bucking expectations and coming through big time for NFL bettors. Here’s a look at three NFL teams that have been getting the job done as live underdogs this season at some popular sports gambling websites:

Cleveland Browns (2-3 SU, 3-2 ATS)
The Browns have covered as underdogs in back-to-back games, losing a heartbreaker on a missed field goal – that hit the upright – 30-27 as 4.5-point pups at San Diego in Week 4 and knocking off the Baltimore Ravens, 33-30 in overtime as 6.5-point road dogs this past Sunday. Cleveland’s other ATS win came as a 1-point favorite hosting Tennessee in Week 2. Quarterback Josh McCown has gone for big yards in the last two contests, making everyone forget about Johnny Manziel and he’s found playmakers in WR Travis Benjamin and RB Isaiah Crowell. The Browns are once again in the underdog role in Week 6, hosting Denver as 4.5-point pups.

New York Giants (3-2 SU, 3-2 ATS)
The Giants failed to come through for backers against the Niners this past Sunday night, set as 7.5-point favorites, but the G-Men have been the best bet when they wear the dog ears. New York has scored two of its ATS wins while getting the points, covering as 7-point underdogs in a Week 1 loss to Dallas and defeating Buffalo as a 6.5-point road underdog in Week 4. The Giants are on the road in Philadelphia in Week 6, and will likely be road pups when lines hit the board.

Washington Redskins (2-3 SU, 3-2 ATS)
Sticking in the NFC East, the Redskins have been underdogs in all three of their paydays so far in 2015. In fact, Washington has been getting the points in all five of its contests heading into Week 6 – including this Sunday’s road trip to take on the Jets. Books have pegged the Skins as 5.5-point underdogs. Washington is playing above the oddsmakers’ expectations and nearly took out undefeated Atlanta on the road in Week 5. Kirk Cousins is gaining the trust of his teammates and the defense has held opponents under 21 points per game.
