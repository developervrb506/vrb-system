College football teams in danger of the letdown in Week 2



  Tweet

Northwestern goes from being the hunter to being the hunted in Week 2




Week 1 of the college football season always provides at least a handful of exciting upsets and the 2015 opening week didn't disappoint. But those stunning victories often leave those underdogs open for a massive letdown spot in Week 2. Here are three teams in danger of this dreaded situational spot this week so you can review before making your football bets online:

Northwestern Wildcats (vs. Eastern Illinois)
The Wildcats shocked Stanford in Week 1, winning 16-6 over the Cardinal as 10-point underdogs. Northwestern goes from being the hunter to being the hunted in Week 2, welcoming FCS Eastern Illinois to Evanston Saturday. Sure, the Wildcats are playing a sub-division school, but the Panthers are coming off an embarrassing loss of their own and will be hungry to get back in the FCS hunt with a win over a Big Ten opponent. The Wildcats are 23.5-point favorites and are 0-3 ATS in their last three games as 20-plus point chalk.

Temple Owls (at Cincinnati)
The Owls took state bragging rights with their first win over football power Penn State in school history in Week 1, making Week 2’s contest with Cincinnati a letdown spot 74 years in the making. Temple turned up the intensity on defense and took a 27-10 win as a 6-point home underdog. The stop unit will be tested versus the Bearcats and stud QB Gunner Kiel, who was third in the AAC on passing in 2014 and tossed for two touchdowns and 233 yards in Cincinnati’s 52-10 thrashing of Alabama A&M in Week 1. The Bearcats opened as high as -7.5 but have since dropped to -6.5, just below the key number of a touchdown.

FIU Golden Panthers (at Indiana)
The Golden Panthers earned a win in their season opener for the first time in four years, upending state rival Central Florida in Week 1 with a 15-14 win, secured by a blocked field goal with 30 second left to go in the fourth quarter. Florida International makes a long trip to Indiana to play the Hoosiers, who are looking for a much better performance than their near loss to Southern Illinois in Week 1. Oddsmakers have pegged the Golden Panthers as 7.5-point road pups, with FIU covering in five of its last six road games.
