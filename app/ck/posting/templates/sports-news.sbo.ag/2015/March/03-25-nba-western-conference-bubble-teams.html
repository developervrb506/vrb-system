NBA Western Conference Bubble Teams



  Tweet

As of Monday, teams ranked No. 1 through No. 7 are pretty much locked into a postseason berth.


Unlike its Eastern Conference counterpart, the Western Conference playoff picture is very picky about who gets in and who stays out. As of Monday, teams ranked No. 1 through No. 7 are pretty much locked into a postseason berth.

That opens up opportunity to find value with the three teams vying for the eighth and final playoff ticket in the West.

Oklahoma City Thunder
The Thunder are the No. 8 team right now and have a bit of a buffer between them and the contenders outside the playoff cut. Oklahoma City continues to put space between itself and the No. 9 and 10 teams despite losing superstar forward Kevin Durant for the season.

Guard Russell Westbrook has put OKC on his shoulders but how much more can he carry? The Thunder split their final 12 games between home and away, with seven of those contests coming against teams currently in the playoffs.

Phoenix Suns
The Suns are still trying to get the taste of missing last year’s postseason out of their mouths and are playing inspired ball heading towards April. Phoenix has won four straight, as of Monday, with quality victories over Dallas, Houston and New Orleans.

The Sun have actually been tightening up on defense, producing a 2-8 Over/Under count in their last 10. They have a daunting schedule ahead, with nine of their final 11 games coming against current playoff teams – six of those on the road.

New Orleans Pelicans
Anthony Davis is making a case for NBA MVP, and if he can get the Pelicans to the playoffs it will be hard to denying him. New Orleans’ star center does it all, leading the team in just about every statistical category. Davis’ two-way play is putting him in the conversation with names like David Robinson and Hakeem Olajuwon.

But that’s the Pelicans’ problem. If Davis doesn’t play at that high level every night, New Orleans can’t compete with the elite in the West. The Pelicans head into this week on a three-game skid and more losses could have this team calling it quits before the end of the month.
