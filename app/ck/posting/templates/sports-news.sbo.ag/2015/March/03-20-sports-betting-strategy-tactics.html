Sports Betting Strategy & Tactics



  Tweet

There are some golden rules to follow when it comes to making a wager.


No matter if you’re just starting your sports betting career or you’re sharp as a tack, there are some golden rules to follow when it comes to making – and hoping winning – a wager. Here a four ways to instantly become a smarter sports bettor:

Money management
Like all gambling, you have to make sure you’re playing within your means. That’s setting a strict bankroll for yourself that you’re comfortable losing – god forbid – and managing that money properly. Professional bettors and bookmakers say you should wager between five and 10 percent of your bankroll on a bet, depending on how strongly you feel about it. If you have a bankroll of $1,000, you should keep your wagers between $50 and $100 dollars.

Know how to read odds
Every bet comes with a price and being able to weigh risk and reward is very important for bettors. Many new handicappers can mismanage their money playing too many heavy favorites, and that chalk comes with a hefty price tag. A -230 favorite means that you have to wager $230 just to win $100. At that rate, you can go 7-4 for a weekend and still be in the hole thanks to those expensive faves.

Get the best line
Most pro bettors have a line already made up before the oddsmakers post their numbers, and bet accordingly once the odds hit the board. If you have an idea of where and when the market will move a line, you can time your play so that you get the best value available. If you like a football favorite, snatching up the -6.5 spread before it jumps to a touchdown can be the difference between winning and losing.

Trends, spots, streaks
The internet age has exposed sports bettors to endless amounts of information. Some good. Some bad. Weeding through which tidbits actually hold value is a process, but bettors should tread lightly with historic trends and focus more on current form. Finding situational angles like letdowns, lookaheads, and tough scheduling spots are a smart practice to follow. And, as always, be very careful betting on winning streaks or losing skids to end. Often times tracking a team’s ATS success on these runs will give an indication of a pending turnaround.
