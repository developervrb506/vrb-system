MLB Postseason Pennant Props



  Tweet

We take a look at each team’s odds at some top sports betting sites to win their respective pennant.


October baseball is finally here. After a long, grueling summer, the MLB playoff matchups are set, beginning with two exciting play-in Wild Card games this week. We take a closer look at each team’s odds at some top sports betting sites to win their respective pennant this postseason:

American League Pennant Odds
Toronto Blue Jays (8/5): Power, pitching and potential. The Blue Jays have it all. What they don’t have is much postseason experience and a manager that may be in over his head as the playoff pressure mounts this October.

Kansas City Royals (9/5): The Royals stumbled toward the end of the season but did enough to hang on to home field. Considering they play a Wild Card team in the ALDS, Kansas City is getting nice odds to win the pennant for the second straight year.

Texas Rangers (7/2): The Rangers did a 180 this season and now must deal with a stacked Toronto team. Texas went 2-4 with a staff ERA of 5.37 in six games with the Jays.

Houston Astros (6/1): It looked like Houston may have been playing itself out of a postseason ticket but a late surge saved the day and may have the Astros riding major momentum into the play-in game.

New York Yankees (8/1): A late-season swoon has New York backing into October, losing six of its final seven games. But, the Yankees do have home field for the play-in.

National League Pennant Odds
Los Angeles Dodgers (2/1): Los Angeles enjoys a travel edge in this series, forcing the Mets to come all the way across the country. The Dodgers picked up some big wins before the end of the season and have one of the most talented lineups.

New York Mets (9/4): The Mets have a sturdy rotation but the offense can go MIA at times. New York skidded into the postseason with just one win in its final six games. It went 4-3 in seven meetings with the Dodgers.

St. Louis Cardinals (29/10): Built for October baseball, the Cardinals have a sterling staff and thrive in close contests, posting a 32-23 record in one-run games and a 46-30 mark versus NL Central foes, which it will face with Chicago and Pittsburgh in the play-in.

Chicago Cubs (5/1): The Cubbies are back in the playoffs, which may set this sad-sack franchise up for a letdown in the play-in. Chicago went 11-8 in the season series with the Bucs.

Pittsburgh Pirates (11/2): The Pirates are worth a flyer at these prices. Pittsburgh is home to Chicago in the play-in and went toe-to-toe with St. Louis with a 9-10 record on the year.
