Beware betting these MLB teams packing it in



  Tweet

Here are three clubs bettors should beware.


There’s a lot of baseball to be played in the major leagues this summer but already teams are showing their hand when it comes to the rest of 2015. Some are pushing for the postseason while others are selling off assets and we won’t be seeing a world series betting line.

Here are three clubs bettors should beware of as they play out the remaining schedule:

Cincinnati Reds
The Reds didn’t wait until the trade deadline to make a move, dealing star pitcher Johnny Cueto to the Kansas City Royals for three pitching prospects. Cincinnati is in the NL Central basement, with a 43-53 record as of Monday and burning through -12.25 betting units so far this season. The Reds may be in full fire sale mode this month, with rumors that veteran Jay Bruce is on the block. Bettors should be wary of Cincy as the club begins to call up youngsters and tinker with its lineup from day to day.

Chicago White Sox
The White Sox swept a four-game set with the Indians this past weekend but it’s a case of too little, too late for the South Siders. Chicago has played better in July but is well off the pace in the competitive AL Central and trade rumors are swirling about like Windy City gusts, with most of the buzz focused on pitchers Jeff Samardzija and Jose Quintana. The ChiSox are 46-50 as of Monday, down -4.54 units for bettors.

Oakland Athletics
The A’s are known for scoring underrated talents, building them up, and then selling them off to the highest bidder. Oakland could be in rebuild mode again after struggling to keep up in the AL West this summer. The Athletics already dealt Scott Kazmir to the Astros and rumors have it that reliever Tyler Clippard and utility man Ben Zobrist are soon on their way out of Oakland. To add a little gas to that fire, the A’s were swept by the rival Giants this past weekend. They enter Monday with a 44-56 record and have burned baseball bettors for -20.85 units – the worst bet in the majors.
