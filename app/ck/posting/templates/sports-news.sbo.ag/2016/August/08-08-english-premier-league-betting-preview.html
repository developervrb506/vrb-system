English Premier League betting preview: Manchester rivals top the board



  Tweet

Here’s a look at the betting favorites to win the English Premier League in 2016-17.


Leicester City became legend in last season’s EPL campaign, cashing in at massive odds to win the Premiership. Soccer oddsmakers aren’t sold on a sequel to that Cinderella story, setting the defending winners as 20/1 long shots this year.

Here’s a look at the betting favorites to win the English Premier League in 2016-17 before you do any sportsbook online betting:

Manchester City +250
City settled for a disappointing fourth place in 2015-16 after winning or placing second the four years prior. Expectations are high again thanks to Manchester City bringing former Barcelona and Bayern Munich manager Pep Guardiola on board. They didn’t make a major splash this offseason like rival United, but did add Ilkay Gündogan, Leroy Sané and Nolito to go along with the talents of Kevin De Bruyne and Sergio Agüero. Consistency is why books are leaning ever so slightly toward City in the EPL futures.

Manchester United +275
José Mourinho on the sidelines is enough to convince oddsmakers that Manchester United is back among the class of the EPL this season – at least on par with rival Manchester City. Plenty of experts have the Red Devils penciled in as champions after boosting their roster with names like Zlatan Ibrahimovic, Eric Bailly, Henrikh Mkhitaryan, and the big-money transfer of Paul Pogba from Juventus. Fans finally have the manager they wanted and a lineup to be proud of, crossing their fingers that these bring stability to a club that has burned through four managers in three seasons.

Arsenal +400
By the looks of the futures board, the 2016-17 season is really a two-horse race. But we all know what happened last year. Arsenal isn’t rolling over for the two Manchester clubs after a second-place finish in 2015-16 but many believe this could be the end of Arsène Wenger’s 20-year tenure with the club – win or lose. However, that familiarity could be the Gunners’ strength with both teams above them in the odds working under new managers. Wenger’s swan song could end with an EPL title, giving his players plenty of motivation this season.
