Big betting profits from college football's smallest schools



  Tweet

These are some of the small schools making big money this NCAA football season.


The best football bets online don’t always come from the best teams. And in college football, rarely do the best bets come from the best conferences. Betting value tends to dry up with programs from Power 5 conferences, simply because everyone is paying attention.

But in small conferences, where there aren’t so many eyes and headlines don’t go national, good football bets tend to stick around week-to-week. These are some of the small schools making big money this NCAA football season.

Buffalo Bulls (3-3 SU, 6-0 ATS)
Buffalo showed flashes of its promising profits in non-conference play when it went toe-to-toe with Minnesota and Army on the road, keeping it close and covering easily at +22.5 and +15 respectively in Weeks 1 and 2. Despite a .500 record the Bulls are one of the tougher teams in the MAC, most recently taking Western Michigan to seven overtimes for a crazy 71-68 loss in which they covered as 7.5-point home underdogs.

Will that heartbreaker set Buffalo up for a letdown in Week 7? The Bulls are back at home as 4.5-point pups hosting Northern Illinois. Football bettors do expect Buffalo to be a little flat, moving this line up from +3.5. The Bulls also have a quarterback controversy brewing with starter Tyree Jackson getting pressure from backup Drew Anderson after Jackson missed the last game with a knee injury. He’s questionable for Saturday’s game against the Huskies.

Fresno State Bulldogs (3-2, 4-0-1 ATS)
The Bulldogs swung big during non-conference play, running into two of last year’s CFP Final Four teams, Alabama and Washington, in Weeks 2 and 3. Needless to say, Fresno State didn’t win outright but it did manage to cover as a massive underdog in both games.

Those tough matchups set the tone for the next two weeks, with FSU defeating Nevada by 20 points (-7.5) and then knocking off San Jose State on the road (pushing as 17-point chalk) in Week 6. Bulldog bettors can thank the team’s defense, which has forced 11 turnovers in five games including four in the win over the Spartans. Fresno State takes on New Mexico as a 2.5-point home favorite Saturday, having covered in each of their last eight home games.

Eastern Michigan Eagles (2-3 SU, 4-1 ATS)
Eastern Michigan received some attention in Week 2 of the NCAA football season when it upset Rutgers as a 5.5-point road underdog. The Eagles followed that game with a tough overtime loss to Ohio at -1.5, for its lone ATS defeat. That close loss has sent EMU on a bit of a slide, but the Eagles are doing enough to keep the covers coming.

To Eastern Michigan’s defense, the team has played three of its last four on the road and will make it four out of five when the Eagles visit Army as 6-point underdogs this weekend. The stop unit is giving up just 18.2 points per game while limiting rushing attacks to an average of 130.6 yards on the ground. That will be put to the test against an Army triple-option that picks up almost 373 rushing yards per game.
