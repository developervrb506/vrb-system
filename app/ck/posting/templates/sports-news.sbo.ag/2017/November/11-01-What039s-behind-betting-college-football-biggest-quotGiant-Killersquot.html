What's behind betting college football biggest "Giant Killers"?



  Tweet

We highlight some of the “giant killers” from this year’s college football season


Upsets are what make college sports so great, and the 2017 NCAA football season is no exception. There have been a number of stunners that have shaken up the college landscape – thanks in large part to these teams below.

We highlight some of the “giant killers” from this year’s college football season and their success against the ncaa football betting lines as the schedule rolls into November.

Iowa State Cyclones (6-2 SU, 7-1 ATS)
What in the Big 12 is happening with the Cyclones? Iowa State is tops in the conference after knocking off Oklahoma as a 31-point road underdog in Week 6, upending Texas Tech as a 7-point road pup in Week 8, then putting TCU’s nation title hopes to bed with a 14-7 win as +7.5 home dogs last Saturday. Those three victories headline a year in which ISU has failed to cover the spread only once: a 17-7 loss to Texas as a 5-point underdog in Week 5.

The Cyclones’ improbable profits are even more unbelievable when you consider the controversy at quarterback for this program. Starter Jacob Park took a leave from the team to work on some personal health issues, leaving a paper-thin QB depth chart to lean on fifth-year walk-on senior Kyle Kempt. He’s undefeated under center for Iowa State, backed by a solid defense that ranks 32nd in yards allowed and just 21st in points against. The Cyclones have a tough challenge in Week 10, traveling to West Virginia as 2.5-point dogs.

Syracuse Orange (4-4 SU, 5-2 ATS)
The Orange were bottom feeders in the ACC since jumping ship to the conference from the Big East a few years back. Syracuse didn’t look great to start the season but didn’t catch fire until Week 4 when it played LSU tough in Death Valley, covering as a 21.5-point underdog. The Orange then went hard at North Carolina State, cashing in again as a large dog. But Cuse didn’t catch the eye of the nation until it knocked off defending national champ Clemson as a 24-point home pup in Week 7.

The Orange followed that win with a loss to Miami, but again played their ACC rivals tough enough to get another win against the spread. Syracuse has not backed down from the conference elite, thanks in part to an improving defense and an efficient offense that sits second in first downs per game in the ACC. Dual-threat QB Eric Dungey is the spark plug of this team, totaling more than 2,700 yards of offense and 20 combined touchdowns.

Arizona State Sun Devils (4-4 SU, 4-4 ATS)
If one team has thrown a wrench into the Pac-12, it’s Arizona State. The Sun Devils made a big splash when it defeated Washington in Week 7 and Utah in Week 8, ruining both of those team’s respective national title hopes. Arizona State also has a win over Oregon as 14.5-point dogs and managed to scare Stanford for an ATS win in Week 5. The Sun Devils were one of the hottest bets in the land, covering in four straight games as double-digit underdogs, but ran into a pissed-off USC squad last week.

Arizona State was squashed 48-17 at home by Southern Cal – a game in which they were only 4.5-point dogs. It seems bookies are catching up with ASU, having it pegged as a 4.5-point home chalk hosting Colorado in Week 10. Those upsets could quickly be a thing of the past, considering the lines have dramatically shifted. The Sun Devils need two more wins to become bowl eligible and only have four games left to do so. Can that added motivation give them value down the stretch?
