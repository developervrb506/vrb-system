Week 11 of the NCAA football betting season will be one to remember



  Tweet

Here’s a breakdown of Saturday’s select contests


The Week 11 NCAA Football slate is shaping up to be one Saturday to remember. Outside of bowl season, it’s rare to see so many big-name matchups. And those marquee games hold a ton of weight as it pertains to the College Football Playoff.

Here’s a breakdown of Saturday’s select contests and their ncaa football betting lines:

Notre Dame Fighting Irish at Miami Hurricanes (+3, 57.5)
These classic rivals are both vying for a spot in the CFP Final Four, with Miami taking the lead in the ACC after Clemson’s stumble and the Fighting Irish boasting just one loss – a one-point defeat at the hands of Georgia. This game will very likely eliminate one of these programs from contention.

Notre Dame has won seven straight since losing to the Bulldogs, boasting a 6-1 mark against the spread in that span. That run is impressive considering the Irish tend to draw a lot of public play which can inflate their weekly pointspreads. Notre Dame has a potent scoring attack, averaging more than 41 points per game, and running back Josh Adams is the key. However, he was limited with headache issues in last week’s game against Wake Forest.

Adams says he’s good to go against a Hurricanes defense that ranks 68th against the run, allowing 172.1 yards on the ground per game. Miami’s defense has been especially stout over its last three games, holding opponents to a grand total of 48 points against in wins over Syracuse, North Carolina and Virginia Tech. On the offensive side of the ball, the Canes will lean heavily on dual-threat QB Malik Rosier to keep the chains moving and pick up gains with his legs when plays break down.

Georgia Bulldogs at Auburn Tigers (+2.5, 47)
This SEC showdown opened as low as a pick’em at some books, with money on the road side swinging the spread nearly a field goal. Georgia brings a ton of momentum into this matchup, boasting a perfect 9-0 record and a 6-3 mark against the spread. Auburn, on the other hand, is home after three straight road games and would love to spoil UGA’s playoff hopes.

The Tigers are fueled by running back Kerryon Johnson, who leads the SEC with 868 rushing yards along with 16 scores. He gets a true test going up against a Bulldogs defense that allows an average of only 86 yards on the ground – fifth fewest in the country. Home field could be worth a lot in this SEC series, with the host going 8-2 ATS in the previous 10 meetings between Georgia and Auburn.

The Bulldogs will aim to control the ground game as well, looking to their dynamic rushing attack. Nick Chubb is fourth in the conference in rushing yards, just ahead of backfield mate Sony Michel, who ranks sixth in the SEC in rushing. That two-headed monster wears down opponents and helps UGA control the tempo, boasting a 33:23.67 average time of possession.

TCU Horned Frogs at Oklahoma Sooners (-6.5, 62.5)
Big 12 heavyweights collide in Norman, where Oklahoma tries to stay on track for the playoff push. The Sooners are coming off a wild rivalry game with Oklahoma State, and could be set up for a bit of a letdown after taking a 62-52 shootout with the Cowboys. Texas Christian is the polar opposite, with a defense giving up only 13.9 points per game. However, if the total is any indication, books see OU dictating the pace.

The Sooners are averaging 51 points over their last three contests and QB Baker Mayfield has the hot hand heading into Week 10. He’s passed for almost 1,300 yards and 11 touchdowns in that stretch but has also thrown four interceptions after recording just one pick in the first six games of the schedule.

The Horned Frogs give up just 214.4 yards through the air per outing and showed they could keep pace with a high-powered conference foe when they knocked off Oklahoma State 44-31 as 9-point underdogs back in September. Texas Christian ran for 238 yards and control the clock for 39:04, keeping the OSU offense off the field. It will likely take a similar approach against Oklahoma Saturday.
