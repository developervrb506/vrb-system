Three college football playoff party poopers to watch and wager



  Tweet

Just one slip up in these final games could cost them a chance at a national title.


The college football season is on the home stretch, which means teams vying for a spot in the College Football Playoff Final Four are walking on egg shells each and every week. Just one slip up in these final games could cost them a chance at a national title.

With that pressure mounting, there are a number of opponents for those CFP contenders who would love to spoil the party – or at least make those top teams sweat a little bit. With that motivation in mind, NCAA football bettors should keep an eye on these CFP party crashers and their ncaa betting lines in the final weeks of the season:

Auburn Tigers
The Tigers would love nothing more than the shake Alabama’s CFP chances to the foundation with a win in the Iron Bowl. Auburn, which ranks sixth in the current CFP standings, has a huge season finale versus the Crimson Tide at home on November 25.

After knocking off Georgia last weekend, the Tigers could very well make their way into the Final Four with a win against Alabama – even with two losses. Auburn won’t get a crack at the SEC Championship Game, so this matchup with Alabama is its last-gasp effort – without anything crazy happening in the conference title game – to make the playoffs. Early lines on this game currently have Auburn as a field-goal underdog for the Iron Bowl.

South Carolina Gamecocks
The Gamecocks couldn’t upend the cart in the SEC but they’ll get a chance to shake things up in the ACC with a finale against rival Clemson in Week 13. South Carolina, which has a FCS matchup with Wofford this weekend, has been one of the better teams in the SEC East, behind only Georgia in terms of the division pecking order.

Clemson, despite a loss to Syracuse, is No. 2 in the CFP rankings but a loss in the season finale would take the Tigers out of the conversation and likely put more weight in the SEC, in terms of getting two teams in the CFP playoffs. South Carolina is sound defensively, allowing only 20.6 points per game, but could have a tough time keeping pace on offense against an eighth-ranked Clemson stop unit. The Gamecocks are 13.5-point home underdogs versus the Tigers in Week 13.

Michigan Wolverines
The Wolverines have put together a solid two-loss season despite a huge migration of top-tier talent to the NFL. Michigan has a chance to scramble the CFP rankings in back-to-back weeks, first facing No. 5 Wisconsin this Saturday then capping the schedule against rival Ohio State, which is sitting No. 9 in the current CFP poll. A Wolverines win over the Badgers would likely be detrimental to the Big Ten’s chances of having a CFP representative, but head coach Jim Harbaugh couldn’t care less about that.

Michigan is a 7.5-point road underdog at Madison, but opened as big as +10 which means early money expects a big effort for the Maize and Blue. The Wolverines have built some momentum against the bottom of the Big Ten the past three games, beating Maryland, Minnesota and Rutgers by a combined score of 103-34, and would relish a chance to spoil playoff hopes for Wisconsin and – even more – Ohio State. Oddsmakers currently have UM as a 10-point home underdog against the Buckeyes on November 25.
