Sportsbettingonline.ag won’t leave bettors thunder struck, honors spread and Over bets for Georgia-UL Monroe game



  Tweet

"So unfortunate. The sportsbook gets put in a position where they end up being the bad guy on both sides of the ball,”



Sportsbettinonline.ag isn’t going to let a little thunder and lightning spoil the first weekend for college football bettors. That’s why the popular online sportsbook has decided to honor and pay out all pointspread bets placed on the Georgia Bulldogs or the UL Monroe Warhawks, even though their Week 1 meeting was cut short due to bad weather.

"These incidents are so unfortunate. The sportsbook gets put in a position where they end up being the bad guy on both sides of the ball,” says Dave Johnson, director for Sportsbettingonline.ag. “There was no way I am going to allow football to start like that for my customers.”


The Bulldogs held a 51-14 lead when officials decided to call the game early into the fourth quarter Saturday, playing just 50 minutes – five minutes short of the industry standard for a “completed game”. Georgia, a 35-point favorite for Saturday’s opener, scored on a 2-yard run from Keith Marshall at 10:58 in the final frame to put UGA up by 37 points – covering the spread by two.

About a minute later, the game was officially called, leaving football bettors in limbo.

Most sportsbooks would rule the game as incomplete and would refund all action, however, Johnson and his experienced team of line managers were keeping a close eye on the UGA-UL Monroe game and felt his customers deserved a break – not the first time his book has honored wagers on a contest that would be normally be ruled “no action” by industry guidelines. And since the score had already topped the 57-point total by the time it was suspended, Johnson also made the call to pay out those bets that took the Over Saturday.

“I am crediting back wins to everyone as a way to say thanks for choosing us this season,” says Johnson. “It will cost the book money but at least we can start off the season on the right foot in the eyes of my customers."

Bettors will recall a similar goodwill gesture from Sportsbettingonline.ag last September when Alabama and Florida Atlantic had their Week 2 meeting cut short due to thunder storms. The Crimson Tide closed as 42.5-point favorites over the Owls and were up 41-0 and driving when officials pulled the plug with 7:53 left in the fourth quarter. Johnson did the same, issuing winners for all those who bet the spread on Alabama or Florida Atlantic.
