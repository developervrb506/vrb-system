Three New Year’s Resolutions for blackjack players



  Tweet

Here are three New Year’s resolutions for players in 2016 to play blackjack online


It’s been two week since the ball dropped on 2015 and flipped the calendar to 2016. So, how’s that New Year’s resolution coming: Haven’t been hitting the gym like you hoped or failed to get the ball rolling on learning a new language? Don’t waste your time kidding yourself – make a resolution that you’ll actually keep and will earn you money.
Here are three New Year’s resolutions players should be making in 2016 before they play blackjack online:

Bet smarter
If you don’t have a system in place for betting, perhaps you should. Blackjack is a game of streaks and skids, and capitalizing on those streaks is paramount to building your bankroll. If you haven’t thought about a wagering strategy that fits you best, why not work with a progressive system? Starting with a set amount, you double the wager each time you win. So a win at $20 will have you betting $40 the next hand, and $80 after that should you win again. Once you lose a hand, you return to the original wager amount.

Try new variations
Blackjack players like what they like. If they play single deck 21, they do so because of the low house edge and ability to operate a fairly reliable card-counting system. That’s not to say they wouldn’t enjoy some of the other variations on the game, such as Switch and Double Exposure blackjack. If anything, set aside a small chunk of your bankroll to expand your gaming menu. It can provide a new way to make money or just be a breath of fresh air after a long session of your regular game.

Keep learning
You may be an experienced blackjack player and know quite a bit about the game, but did Michael Jordan stop practicing once he won his first NBA Championship? Hell no. Like any game, practice makes perfect, so study new strategies and review old ones. And don’t be afraid to play “just for fun” or low-limit games to sharpen your skills or try out new systems. You can be good, but you can always be b.
