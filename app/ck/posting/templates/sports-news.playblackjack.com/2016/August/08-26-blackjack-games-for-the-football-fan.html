Blackjack games for the football fan



  Tweet

Here are three variations of free blackjack online


There’s a crisp bite to the air at the end of the summer that lets us know fall is on the way. And with that change in seasons brings the start of football in the pro and college ranks. Everyone loves football – it’s a scientific fact – including blackjack players. If you love the pigskin and playing 21, here are three variations of free blackjack online that will have you dancing in the end zone.

Single deck blackjack
For those football fans who love the battle of the brains between head coaches, single deck blackjack is for you. There’s a big advantage to having a card-counting strategy – just like a solid game plan – since probabilities are much more accurate when playing with just 52 cards. Players can read the table, calculate the probability of the next card out of the shoe, and bet accordingly. It’s a battle between you and the house, just like two great coaches going toe-to-toe with their playbooks.

Switch blackjack
Football is all about making adjustments. The quarterback steps to the line of scrimmage, reads the defense, recognizes the blitz, and calls an audible to change the play. Changing your play is the name of the game with switch blackjack. This popular variation deals two hands to the player and allows them to switch the top card in order to create stronger hands against the dealer’s one. Knowing when to switch is key to cashing in, just like a QB calling a hot route when he sees a weakness in the defense.

Double exposure blackjack
Victories come on Sunday but they start on Monday. Being prepared is the first step in winning, and for football teams that means scouting your opponents through game film and knowing which move they’re going to make next. Double exposure blackjack gives you that same advantage, but without hours of film study, forcing the dealer to show both cards to the player. That means players can hit or stand accordingly, knowing exactly what the dealer needs to beat them, and be aggressive when the dealer is showing a strong hand.
