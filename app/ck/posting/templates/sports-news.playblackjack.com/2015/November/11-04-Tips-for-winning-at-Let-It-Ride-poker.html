Tips for winning at Let It Ride poker



  Tweet

Let It Ride poker here are some handy tips and poker strategy –Let It Rid–


Let It Ride arguably has the best name of any poker variation. Not only is it catchy and fun to say – imagine screaming “Let it ride!” in the casino – but it’s also pretty explanatory when it comes to the rules of the game. If you’re looking to try Let It Ride poker for the first time or just want to polish up on some do’s and don’ts, here are some handy tips and poker strategy for playing – and winning – Let It Ride:

Don’t be intimidated
At first glance, Let It Ride poker looks like it might be too rich for most bettors’ blood. You do place three wagers at once – all of equal value – but really is your original ante bet the only money on the table. As the name of the game would infer, players can either let the second and third wagers “ride” or they can pull them back, depending on the strength of your hand. So if this is your first time looking at Let It Ride, don’t be put off by the way the bets are placed.

When to ride
The great thing about Let It Ride is that you can really cash in when you have a great hand, rather than just winning a standard bet. Because you have three bets in play, getting three solid cards to start won’t go to waste. Players are recommended to “Let it ride” if they have: a pair of 10s or better, any three cards of a Royal Flush, three to a straight flush, and three to a straight.

Outside and inside
When chasing a straight in Let It Ride, there are two ways to separate the strategy. If you have three or four cards that could make a straight but are missing cards inside – like drawing a 6,7,9,10 – you then need an inside card – in this case an eight. If you drew a four-card hand of 7,8,9,10, you then need cards outside of the straight – in this case a six or a Jack will complete the straight. Outside straights are easier to complete, so be more aggressive chasing those rather than trying to make an inside straight.
