Adjusting strategies for different types of blackjack



  Tweet

Not all blackjack games can be approached the same way


Blackjack has become such a popular casino option, online and at brick-and-mortar spots, that it’s spawned a number of variations on the classic game of 21. But not all blackjack games can be approached the same way. Here’s a brief rundown of the suggested strategies for some of the more popular blackjack variations when you play blackjack online:

Single-deck/8-deck
The traditional game of blackjack lends itself to those who love to crunch the numbers for probability. Card counting systems and many golden rules – like hitting on a soft 17 – come into play when trying to get one up on the house. The preferred counting system for many players is the high/low method, which basically puts and positive, negative or zero value on certain cards, allowing the player to quickly add up the values of the visible cards and stand or hit accordingly.

Blackjack switch
This popular twist on traditional blackjack has you playing two hands at once and allows players the opportunity to swap cards between the two hands. Many of the tips and suggestions for traditional blackjack hold true in switch, however, when considering a swap make sure you aren’t breaking up a winning hand just to have the chance of winning both. If you draw a 20 in one hand, leave it alone and play the remaining hand as a normal blackjack hand. Also, be cautious when it comes to switching into hands of 17 if the dealer is showing a 10-value card. They have a number of options – 7, 8, 9, 10, Jack, Queen, King and Ace – out there to beat your hand.

Double exposure
The big twist with double exposure blackjack is that the player is able to see both of the dealer’s cards, rather than just the one in traditional blackjack. This is a huge advantage to the player so the house offsets that edge by giving all pushes to the dealer – except on a tied blackjack which pays even money. Due to that wrinkle, players must take upon a much more aggressive strategy when it comes to hitting on higher hand counts and can be far less aggressive on low hand counts when the dealer is showing a hand that will likely bust, such as a 16.
