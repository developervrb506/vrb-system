Know how to best bet in blackjack



  Tweet

Here four different casino blackjack tips when playing blackjack


Just like counting cards and predicting what’s next to come out of the shoe, blackjack players should also have a strategy when it comes to placing their bets. Here are four different casino blackjack tips and their benefits and disadvantages when playing blackjack:

Flat wagering
This is the most common betting practice, especially as it pertains to newcomers and casual bettors. Flat wagering is simply betting the same amount each and every hand, no matter if you’ve won or lost the previous hand. It’s a very simple method to manage and works well if you have a tight bankroll with little wiggle room to play with. However, it doesn’t take advantage of any hot streaks that may come your way.

Martingale System
It may have a fancy name but this is simply chasing your losses with a bigger bet. As an example, if a player bets $100 and loses, they will try to make up for that loss by betting $200 on the next hand. If that bet loses, they double up again and wager $400. Once they hit a winning hand, they go back to betting that original rate. This is a quick way to recoup losses, but if you hit a cold streak it can clean out your bankroll in a hurry. You have to have the ample funds to back up the Martingale System, which takes a lot of money and a lot of guts.

High/Low
High/Low is a cross between flat wagering and the Martingale system, however, it works in reverse. Players will wager a set amount – the low - and if they win, they will go up to the high. As an example, a blackjack player wagering $100 might bump up to $200 following a win and play that high wager until they lose, then go back to the low wager. It’s a good way to maximize your money when you hit a hot streak without risking a big loss if things go sour.

Progressive
This is the betting strategy of choice for many professionals and high-rollers. Players will make an initial wager – for example $50 – and following a win may up that bet to $75. After two straight wins, the wager may bump to $150, then $200 or $300. It takes a lot of money to run with this strategy, which is why it is reserved to serious bettors, but it does maximize your wins and makes a loss – even at a large amount – sting much less than playing the Martingale System.
