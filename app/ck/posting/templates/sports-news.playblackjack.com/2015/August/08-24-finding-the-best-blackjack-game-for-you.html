Finding the best blackjack game for you



  Tweet

Popularity comes from a long list of variations on the game.


Blackjack remains one of the most popular casino games available, no matter if you’re playing for fun or betting big at the tables. And with that popularity comes a long list of variations on the game. The vast menu of blackjack options allows players to find the perfect flavor of blackjack to fit their personalities. Here’s a look at these options when playing free blackjack online:

Advantageous – Single-Deck Blackjack
If you’re always hunting for a deal or you’re just sick of losing money to the casino, single-deck – also known as single-hand – blackjack carries one of the lowest house edges you’ll find with any casino game. Single-deck blackjack, which uses just one 52-card deck compared to multiple-deck games, has a 1.5 percent house advantage and is much easier to predict, given the smaller amount of variables with each hand.

Strategic – Six-Deck Blackjack
Got a knack for numbers and always have a plan up your sleeve? If so, six-deck blackjack offers those with solid mathematical skills a good challenge while also paying out more than most other forms of blackjack. Card-counting systems, like high/low, allow players to track and predict which cards will be coming out of the shoe next.

Flighty – Switch Blackjack
If you have a tough time making up your mind or just like to keep your options open, switch blackjack will allow you enjoy basic blackjack action but with a twist. Switch blackjack deals two hands to players and allows them to swap out the top cards – or second cards in the hand – to create a better blackjack count against the dealer’s single hand.

Excitable – Spanish Blackjack
Do bells and whistles get you pumped up? Do you love a little something extra? Spanish blackjack has grown a loyal following thanks in part to its bonuses not found in other forms of blackjack. The game is played with a 48-card deck – taking out the four 10s – and players are rewarded for different combos of 21. Making 21 with a 6-7-8 or 7-7-7 and having those cards suited can pay out big.

Gambler – Double Exposure
Well, if you’re playing blackjack to begin with, you’ve got a little gambler in you already. However, some blackjack players have it more than others. That’s where double exposure blackjack comes in, offering players a look at both of the dealer’s cards instead of just one. The house offsets this advantage with giving pushes to the dealer and paying even money on blackjack, which means players must be a little more aggressive when it comes splitting cards and hitting on higher hands.
