Blackjack’s biggest sins



  Tweet

Doubling down in blackjack should only be reserved to select opportunities



Like any casino game, there is a clear strategy when it comes to playing blackjack. However, these unwritten rules are often times forgotten by players or not recognized by newbies. Consider this your warning. Here are some casino blackjack tips for some of blackjack’s biggest sins:


Splitting Tens
If the dealer is generous enough to give you a 20 - be it with 10s, Jacks, Queens or Kings - don’t soil a winning hand by getting greedy and trying to double your luck. Some players new to blackjack see spitting 10s as a chance to score two winning hands. However, if you’re holding two high cards off the deal, chances are there are more lower-value cards coming out of the shoe. A 20 can only be beaten by a blackjack, so recognize that probability and just smile and take your winnings.

Not hitting on soft 17
In the game of blackjack, an Ace is valued at 1 or 11. That means having an Ace in your first two cards gives you a bit of a safety net when it comes to hitting. Most dealers must stand on 17, which means if you are looking at an Ace/Six combo, you hold a hand worth 17 or 7. Having that option opens the door to a hit on soft 17 with a good chance of improving your hand or having a shot at topping the dealer’s 17.

Dumb doubling down
Doubling down in blackjack should only be reserved to select opportunities, like when looking at a 10 or 11. There’s a good chance you will pull a 10 or face card with your next hand, creating a 20 or 21 which is pretty much guaranteed money. However, players can sometimes get ahead of themselves and double down on hands they have no business throwing more money at. And even doubling down on 10 or 11 when the dealer is showing nine or 10 can lead to some crushing losses, made extra worse by the fact you threw twice the money on it.
