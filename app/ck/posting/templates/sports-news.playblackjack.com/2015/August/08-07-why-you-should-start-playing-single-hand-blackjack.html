Why you should start playing Single-Hand Blackjack



  Tweet

The variation of choice for the player looking for the best odds is Single Hand Blackjack


 

Blackjack is arguably the most popular casino game, whether you’re sitting at an actual table or playing online. The variation of choice for the player looking for the best odds is Single Hand Blackjack, which uses a single 52-card deck compared to the common 6-deck game you’ll find at most casinos.
Here’s some casino blackjack tips that will convince you why you should try your hand at Single Hand Blackjack.

Fewer cards
Since Single Hand is played with a solo 52-card deck, there are fewer variants when it comes to what card is next out of the shoe. Basic card-counting strategies hold up in Single Hand, with the player knowing a table full of 10s and face cards should leave plenty of low-value cards waiting to come. lowering the chances of a bust. And it works the other way around, knowing a table of smaller value cards means there are some higher cards on the way and the risk of going bust is extremely high.

Bigger limits, lower edge
If you’re seeking out an advantageous casino game, you want to get the most bang for your buck. Single Hand Blackjack carries a house edge of just 1.5 percent, which is among the lowest advantages you give up to casino operators. Due to those slim margins, Single Hand often requires a larger wager from players compared to six-deck games. Most limits start at $5 and go as high as the casino is comfortable offering the player. If you’re a savvy blackjack player, you welcome these higher stakes and gladly trade them for better value.

Good for learning
As mentioned above, Single Hand Blackjack really offers itself up to card-counting strategies such as high/low. It’s much more predictable than a six-deck or higher game, allowing beginners and those ironing out their card counting system to grasp the concepts. Often times in six-deck games, even the most calculated counting can be trumped by the randomness coming out of the shoe. Single Hand eases players into the game before moving on to more challenging blackjack variations.
