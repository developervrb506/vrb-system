Want a wild wager? Jump over the pond and bet these Grand National futures odds



  Tweet

Oddsmakers' favorites to finish up front at Aintree Racecourse this spring


In North America, we have the Kentucky Derby as the biggest horse racing bets event of the year. Across the pond, in the United Kingdom, the Grand National is a headliner of the horse racing calendar with just about every punter holding a ticket on the annual event at Aintree Racecourse near Liverpool.

This isn’t your standard horse race – this is hunt racing. Horses are running a 4-mile track and must jump over hedges and fences, with the Grand National boasting a field of up to 40 competitors. On top of that, the best horses in the field are forced to run with weights as to handicap the favorites. Over the course of the race, riders are bucked, horses are tripped, and the number of riders that finish the race is drastically diminished.

The 2018 Grand National doesn’t take place until April but you can get down action on the top contenders vying for that £1,000,000 purse. Here are the oddsmakers' favorites to finish up front at Aintree Racecourse this spring:

Blaklion (+1,000)
Blaklion has won nine of his career 24 runs and has finished inside the Top 3 in 17 of those events. The nine-year-old colt won his most recent effort, finishing first in a field of 15 horse in the Randox Health Becher Handicap Chase in early December.

That result headlines a 2017 in which Blaklion placed second in two other events – Charlie Hall Chase and the Grand National Trial Handicap Chase – and placed fourth in the 2017 Grand National. He’s gradually run longer races in 2017 and should be in fine form to face the 34 furlongs in the 2018 Grand National in April.

Native River (+1,600)
Native River was raced lightly in 2017, featured in only two events last year. The eight-year-old finished first in the Denman Chase but has been absent since a third-place finish at the Cheltenham Gold Cup Chase in March. Trainer C.L. Tizzard has recently been running Native River in preparation for the 2018 schedule.

For his career, Native River has finished first nine times in 19 races and has placed within the Top 3 in 16 of those showings. His longest run was 31 furlongs at the Year ff The National Hunt Chase Challenge in 2016, placing second in a field of 20 horses. With his workload increasing before the spring, Native River should be ready to run up front in the 2018 Grand National.

Minella Rocco (+2,000)
This eight-year-old from Ireland was very busy in 2017, running five times and finishing inside the Top 3 just once – that being a second-place showing in the Cheltenham Gold Cup Chase. He's had some bad luck, pulling up in the BetVictor.com Handicap Chase and having his rider unseated in the Stan James Irish Gold Cup. Trainer Jonjo O'Neill isn’t too concerned with his horse’s recent skid and liked the way Minella Rocco kept competing in his most recent run in the Leopardstown Christmas Chase, in which he finished fourth.

For his career Minella Rocco has won only three of his 14 overall races, finished second twice and third twice. His longest challenge was the 31 furlongs at the Year of The National Hunt Chase Challenge Cup where he placed first in the field of 20 horse, edging Native River for the victory. He finished second in the Gold Cup last year and presents great value at the 20/1 price tag for the 2018 Grand National.
