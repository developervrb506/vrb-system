Prix De Larc Triomphe betting preview



  Tweet

Across the pond the next horse race is the famed Prix De Larc Triomphe taking place at Longchamp Racecourse, France on October 4.


 

All eyes in North America are on American Pharoah and his bid to win horse racing’s Grand Slam at the upcoming Breeders’ Cup Classic on Halloween, but across the pond the next big race on the betting docket for anyone betting on horse races is the famed Prix De Larc Triomphe, which takes place at Longchamp Racecourse in Paris, France on October 4.

The field for this event is still rounding into form, most recently shrinking with Postponed, who was at 16/1, pulled from the race by owner Sheikh Mohammed Obaid earlier this week. How does this move impact the odds for the Prix De Larc Triomphe? Here’s a look at the favorites to win Europe's most prestigious horse race:

Treve (Even)
Treve is looking to make history at the “Arc” after winning this race the previous two years. She’s the first horse to win back-to-back Prix De Larc Triomphe titles since Alleged in 1978 and could be the only one to complete the hat trick if oddsmakers are right with their current price. Treve is now a five-year-old but doesn’t show any signs of slowing down, winning all three of her 2015 events including the Prix Vermeille-G1 at Longchamp earlier this month. This is her course and her race. Which would make anything but a first-place finish a huge upset in the eyes of European horse bettors.

New Bay (+500)
The French Derby winner has been putting in some solid training session in preparation for the Arc. New Bay is trained by Andre Fabre, who has won a record seven Prix De Larc Triomphe titles, and he was impressed with his horse’s win at the Prix Niel in mid-September. He’s showing great speed at the 1 ½-mile distance and has seen his odds trimmed significantly since, dropping from as high as 8/1 following his latest victory.

Golden Horn (+600)
The English horse looks to make a splash on the big French stage in early October. Golden Horn, a three-year-old colt trained by John Gosden (who won the Breeders’ Cup Classic with Raven's Pass in 2008), won the Epsom Derby and Eclipse Stakes earlier this year before taking the Irish Champion Stakes this month, edging Free Eagle thanks to a late bump that the officials let stand. While that may tarnish his win a bit, the horse did have to run in the slop after heavy rain the night before made the course at Leopardstown Racecourse a mess.
