Keen Ice and two other horses that could win the Breeders’ Cup Classic



  Tweet

Can he do the same at Keeneland in October? Keen Ice contenders for the 2015 Breeders’ Cup Classic


 

Keen Ice finally edged American Pharoah after finishing behind the Triple Crown champion in the Belmont Stakes and the Haskell Invitational. He sat back and watched American Pharoah and Frosted battle in the Travers Stakes, making his move on the home stretch and blasting this way to the finish line. Can he do the same at Keeneland in October? We know you love to be betting on horse races so we will break down Keen Ice and two other contenders for the 2015 Breeders’ Cup Classic:

Keen Ice (10/1)
Keen Ice seemed to be peaking at the right time when he rushed to the front of the pack in the final furlong of the Travers Stakes. He had a third-place finish in the Belmont Stakes in June and was 2 ¼ lengths back of American Pharoah at the Haskell Invitational in early August before breaking around the pack for the win at Saratoga. He’s shown that he’s on American Pharoah’s level and at times looks to be taking a page from AP’s playbook. But October 31 is a long way off and Keen Ice may cool off before then.

Gleneagles (8/1)
This Irish horse has been great as a three-year-old, winning the 2000 Guineas, Irish 2000 Guineas and the St James's Palace Stakes at Royal Ascot in June. Unlike his American competition, Gleneagles will likely run another race before the Breeders’ Cup Classic, so he’ll be in solid form come October 31. Politics may take him out of the field though, as his owners Coolmore also own the breeding rights to American Pharoah. That conflict of interest could erase a great head-to-head international race.

Texas Red (10/1)
Texas Red finished fifth at the Travers Stakes, following a win at the Jim Dandy-G2 earlier in the month. He put himself among American Pharoah’s rivals when he finished third to the eventual Triple Crown winner at the FrontRunner (G1) Stakes last September then backed that up with a victory in the Breeders' Cup Juvenile, while AP was sidelined with injury. Fates flipped as three-year-olds, with Texas Red missing time with a foot abscess and American Pharoah making history. Trainer Keith Desormeaux has a bit of a chip on his shoulder and would love to finally knock off AP in the Classic.
