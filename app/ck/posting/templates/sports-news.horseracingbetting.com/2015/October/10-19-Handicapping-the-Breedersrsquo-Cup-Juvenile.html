Handicapping the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile



  Tweet

Here the favorites to run up front at Keeneland on Halloween at some horse racing betting sites


 

October is almost half way through, which means the Breeders’ Cup is almost here. This week, we take a look at the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile, which is headlined by some of the very best two-year-old horses in the world – a many of those will go on to race in the Triple Crown events next spring. Here are the favorites to run up front at Keeneland on Halloween at some horse racing betting sites:

Nyquist (2/1)
Nyquist is a sizable favorite for the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile and is the front runner for the early 2016 Kentucky Derby odds as well. He’s dominated his two-year-old campaign, winning each of his four races including the Frontrunner G1 at San Anita which he won by three-quarters of a length over Swipe. Nyquist is the son of Uncle Mo, who also went undefeated in his two-year-old season, and is Kentucky raised, so this could be his big homecoming/coming-out party.

Air Vice Marshal (6/1)
This Aidan O'Brien-trained horse has jumped up the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile odds. He finished second in the Superlative Stakes back in July and won his first race at Gowran Park in Ireland the month before. He was supposed to run in the Champagne Stakes at Doncaster in the UK but didn’t, leaving him with little competition heading into the Breeders’ Cup weekend. However, he is expected to not only run in the Juvenile on Saturday but also the Juvenile Turf G1 on Friday.

Brody’s Cause (6/1)
This horse turned some heads with a strong win at the Claiborne Breeders’ Futurity in early October, taking down favorite Exaggerator on some less-than-ideal conditions at Keeneland – something that should be noted if the weather gets wet on October 31. Brody’s Cause won at Churchill Downs in September and finished eighth at Ellis Park back in August. He likes to run in the clear so keep a close eye on Brody’s Cause and his starting gate for the Juvenile. If he’s pinned inside, he may not get the run jockey Corey Lanerie wants.
