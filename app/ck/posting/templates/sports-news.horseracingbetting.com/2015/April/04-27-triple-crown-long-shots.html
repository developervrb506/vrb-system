Triple Crown Long Shots



  Tweet

What if a relatively unknown horse could come out of the shadows and become the first Triple Crown champion


As the Kentucky Derby draws closer, the “Run for the Roses” and subsequent Triple Crown hopes fall on three horses: American Pharoah, Carpe Diem, and Dortmund. However, what if a relatively unknown horse could come out of the shadows and become the first Triple Crown champion since Affirmed in 1978?

Racing fans can find tremendous value in the Triple Crown odds with these four long shots:

Mubtaahij
The overseas phenom who won on a dirt track at the UAE Derby is hoping that success translates across the pond heading into the Kentucky Derby. He’s shown the ability to turn it on down the stretch and run at the distance needed to win the Derby. The biggest hang-up for Mubtaahij, who is from Dubai, is how he adjusts to life in the United States, already dealing with much colder temperatures and a strange diet that abides by U.S. regulations.

Frosted
Trainers Kiaran McLaughlin is known for making something out of nothing, with long shot Closing Argument finishing second in the 2005 Kentucky Derby. McLaughlin has a similar feeling with Frosted heading to Churchill Downs. He won the Wood Memorial by two lengths at Aqueduct in early April after switching to jockey Joel Rosario and undergoing an overhaul in training regimen. Will those adjusts continue to make a difference?

International Star
The Louisiana Derby winner is very dependent on his draw for the Kentucky Derby and other Triple Crown races, with jockey Miguel Mena preferring to get to the inside and ride the rail – which has never been a promising position when it comes to the Kentucky Derby. But, if he find the gate position he likes, with some help from lady luck, International Star has the chops to contend and has done nothing but win during the prep schedule.

Upstart
No horse is branded with a better name to shake up the Triple Crown picture than this Rick Violette trainer contender. Upstart won the Holy Bull Stakes at Gulfstream Park and also crossed the wire first at the Fountain of Youth Stakes but was disqualified and bumped to second for impending would-be win Itsaknockout. That incident may have hurt the horses odds for the Kentucky Derby and Triple Crown, but it also give added value to Upstart as a live long shot.
