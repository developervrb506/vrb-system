Never too soon to start capping the 2016 Breeders’ Cup Classic



  Tweet

Start capping the 2016 Breeders’ Cup Classic


 

 

American Pharoah completed horse racing’s Grand Slam, winning the Triple Crown – Kentucky Derby, Preakness Stakes and Belmont Stakes – as well as the 2015 Breeders’ Cup Classic this past weekend.

And even though his historic footprints still lay entrenched in the mud at Keeneland Race Course in Lexington, Kentucky, oddsmakers have opened betting on the 2016 Breeders’ Cup Classic, which will take place at Santa Anita Park in California. Here’s an early look at the favorites and their odds to win the final big race of the season before you make your horse racing bets:

Beholder (+500)
Racing fans didn’t get the Classic they wanted when Beholder was scratched from the 2015 field, nixing a showdown with American Pharoah. Would Beholder have challenged the Triple Crown champion? We’ll never know. But bettors may get another shot at betting on Beholder – the famed four-year old mare, who dominated her 2015 schedule – if her owners decide to make a run at becoming the first horse to win three different Breeders’ Cup events.

Shared Belief (+500)
Shared Belief missed most of 2015 after suffering a fractured hip at the Charles Town Classic in April but trained for the first time since this week, taking some rehab runs at Golden Gate Field. The four year old has won 10 of his 12 career starts and finished fourth in the Breeders’ Cup Classic back in 2014. That finish and his ill-fated run at Charles Town are his only two blemishes.

Tonalist (+1,200)
The early odds take a sharp turn outside of the above favorites. Tonalist was among the favorites to win the 2015 Breeders’ Cup Classic, but instead finished a disappointing fifth place. However, unlike most horses, he isn’t done running in 2015. Tonalist is expected to show up at Aqueduct for the Cigar Mile Handicap in late November. Depending on how that run goes, these odds could come down a little. The biggest obstacle facing Tonalist is competing on tracks outside of Belmont Park, where he’s won five of his sixth career races.

Other notables: Honor Code (+1,400), Toast of New York (+1,400), Keen Ice (+2,500)
