Horse racing handicapping tips



  Tweet

If you like the look of a horse but are weary of his past performances, dig deeper.


 

There's an art to handicapping horse races that goes beyond the ability to read the racing forum. Sharp horse bettors keep their ears to the ground when it comes to picking a winner, looking at a number of factors before placing their bets. Here's a look at a few simple “ but subtle“ tips to help you take on the track and win big at horse race betting:

Past performances
Racing forums will give you a bare-bones idea of how a horse has run in his previous events, but doesn't break down just how the race played out. Sometimes a favorite to run up front can draw a tough gate number or get caught up at the start. And often times, conditions aren't perfect leaving a horse slowed by a slick course. If you like the look of a horse but are weary of his past performances, dig deeper and find out why a 3/1 favorite finished fifth in its previous running. Chances are you can discover some influential factors that may or may not be in play this time around.

Tendencies
Horses are funny animals who know what they like. Some thrive when battling their way from the back of the pack, saving their energy for a second-half push, while others love the open air and want to quickly make their way to the lead. There are horses who prefer the inside and others that like to run outside. Knowing a horse's tendencies and factoring in their starting gate position, and how that plays into those plans, will give you an idea of how comfortable a horse will be in a particular race.

Jockeys
Jockeys also have their own tendencies when it comes to race strategy. An experienced jockey will game plan around their ride, so knowing how a horse runs and how it matches up with the man on top is key. Also keep an eye on new jockey/horse pairings and any last-minute rider switches. There's often a period of transition while they get to know each other. Veteran jockeys can also have a calming influence on their mount, especially when it comes to larger stakes races. A horse can get jittery and nervous in a bigger field with a large and loud crowd at the track. Experienced riders have a way of keeping their horse calm and focused.
