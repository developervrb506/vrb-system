Three live long shots to watch at the Breeders’ Cup



  Tweet

All the hype and hoopla heading into the 2015 Breeders’ Cup Classic


All the hype and hoopla heading into the 2015 Breeders’ Cup Classic is about whether or not Triple Crown champion American Pharoah can complete horse racing’s Grand Slam with a win at Keeneland this October.

That has distracted horse racing bettors and oddsmakers when it comes to the Classic futures. There are some serious contenders behind American Pharoah as well as fellow front runners Shared Belief and California Chrome. Here’s a look at three live long shot who could stun the field at the Breeders’ Cup:

Palace Malice (10/1)
The 2013 Belmont Stakes winner has had a very quiet 2015, racing just once at the Diablo Stakes in May, finishing third. A foot injury and a tendon infection has sidelined Palace Malice for the bulk of the season but he did return to training in early July, running at Saratoga Springs. And let’s not pass up the fact that this is a Todd Pletcher-trained horse, which always gives him a chance.

Dortmund (12/1)
In any other year, Dortmund very well could have won the Kentucky Derby or Preakness Stakes. But with American Pharoah leaving waste to his competition and trainer Bob Baffert focusing his energy on his Triple Crown winner, Dortmund just didn’t pop like he was supposed to. That’s not to say he couldn't get his revenge in the Breeders’ Cup Classic. Baffert-trained Bayern won the Classic last season after some early-season misses, and we saw this horse take on all comers before the Triple Crown events.

Hoppertunity (12/1)
A photo finish at the Gold Cup in Santa Anita left Hoppertunity edged by Hard Aces last month, but his impressive display on the 1 ¼-mile track – the same distance as the Classic – should have horse bettors taking notice. Hoppertunity never got his stab at glory as a three year old, getting scratched as the second favorite in the 2014 Kentucky Derby due to soreness. And he hasn’t made the field for any big-name events since. But this horse has won, placed or shown in nine of 11 career races and could be anxious to show what he can do on the big stage in Keeneland this fall.
