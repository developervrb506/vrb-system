Basic Blackjack Tips


 

Poker is one of the most popular table games found in live and online casinos, and a variation that’s quickly picking up a loyal following is Pai Gow poker. This unique twist on the classic Chinese dominoes game is a great option for those looking to get away from the standard grind of video poker.

Here’s some basics to get you started:

Seven Cards, Two Hands
Unlike standard poker, where you make the best hand you can from a five-card draw, Pai Gow is played with seven cards and players must make their best two hands from that in order to beat the dealer, who is also dealt seven cards. Standard poker ranks are used to determine which hand is the high hand (or best hand) and which will be their remaining hand. If a player has a five-card rank – like a straight or flush – that means they only have two remaining cards to make their second hand.

PokerRankings
Just like other poker games, standard poker rankings apply to Pai Gow. If a player is dealt a seven-card hand with King, Eight, King, Ten, Six, Five, Ten, they have two solid poker hands with a pair of Kings as their high hand and a pair of Tens as their second hand. If the dealer is dealt a seven-card hand of Three, Ace, Queen, Three, Seven, Five, Ace, then their high hand is the pair of Aces – which beats the player’s pair of Kings – and the second hand is a pair of Threes – which loses to the player’s pair of Tens. In this case, since both parties won and lost a hand, the game is ruled a push.

Win, Lose, Push
The trick with Pai Gow is that the player must win both hands to win their bet, and lose both hands to lose the bet. If the player and dealer split a win and loss, the game is ruled a push and bets are refunded. This can often make Pai Gow a much slower-paced and relaxing game but it will also test a poker player’s patience and concentration.
