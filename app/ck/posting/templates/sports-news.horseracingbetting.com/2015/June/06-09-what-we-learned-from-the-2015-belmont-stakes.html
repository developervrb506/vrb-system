What We Learned From The 2015 Belmont Stakes



  Tweet

Maybe one of the most interesting stories of the weekend was the fact that $315,829 in winnings were left behind at the Belmont Stakes


So the drought is over as American Pharaoh has won the Triple Crown. Maybe one of the most interesting stories of the weekend was the fact that $315,829 in winnings were left behind at the Belmont Stakes. Horse race betting enthusiasts left behind 90,237 $2 bets on American Pharaoh at the track with the winning tickets simply left lying around. At any rate, here are a couple of the most important things we learned from the 2015 Belmont Stakes:

American Pharoah Will Be The Favorite For The Breeders' Cup Classic
Owner Ahmed Zayat has already stated that American Pharoah will race again in 2015, and the options are the Jim Dandy Stakes on August 1st, the Haskell Invitational on August 2nd, the Pacific Classic on August 22nd and the Travers on August 29th. Those are all fine and good, but the big target has to be the Breeders' Cup Classic on Halloween as it carries a $5 million purse and after the Kentucky Derby, it is the biggest race on the American thoroughbred horse racing calender. And when it is announced that he'll line up at Keeneland on October 31st, he will be the favorite for the Classic.

This Will Probably Happen Again Very Soon
There was 37 years between Affirmed and American Pharoah winning the Triple Crown but there probably won't be another 37-year wait as these things tend to go in cycles. Prior to Affirmed, the great Secretariat had won it in 1973 and then Seattle Slew in 1977, so that is three times in five years. Horse race betting handicappers know that the biggest difference between now and then is that there is more competition, more high-level horses, so it is tougher to separate one from the pack like American Pharoah did. But don't be surprised if there is another Triple Crown winner in the next 3-5 years.
