Will there be another Triple Crown winner in 2016?



  Tweet

Could this horses follow in American Pharoah’s big horse shoes


 

American Pharoah ended a 37-year drought for horse racing this spring, becoming the first Triple Crown champion high for the new crop of three-year-olds heading to Churchill Downs for the first weekend in since Affirmed in 1978. It put the sport back in the spotlight and set the bar May.

Funny enough, Affirmed was the first and last time there were back-to-back Triple Crown winners, with his championship coming on the heels of Seattle Slew in 1977. With that in mind before you do any online horse race betting think about these three possible Triple Crown contenders who could follow in American Pharoah’s big horse shoes:

Nyquist
This horse is the current front runner to win the 2016 Kentucky Derby but is anything but a runaway favorite. Nyquist is set at 10/1 coming off a two-year-old campaign that saw him claim victories in all five of his races. He capped the year with an impressive victory in the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile, however, that race has never been a good barometer for Triple Crown success. Nyquist did win the Frontrunner Stakes and the Del Mar Futurity this year – two races that American Pharoah also had on his two-year-old resume.

Exaggerator
Exaggerator won three of his six starts as a two-year-old, with a second, fourth and fifth-place showing in the other events. The most recent was a victory at the Delta Downs Jackpot as a 2/1 favorite, posting a career-best finish which will give him and trainer Keith Desormeaux momentum heading into the Triple Crown prep schedule. He’s a horse that likes to roll with the pack then explode to the front, which can sometimes get him in trouble if he has a rough start – as he did in the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile. In the Delta Down Jackpot he showed his breakaway speed, jumping to the front and holding there for the win.

Mor Spirit
When sizing up potential Triple Crown winners, the trainer is a significant factor. And no trainer has more Triple Crown experience that Bob Baffert right now. Coming off a Triple Crown campaign in 2015 with American Pharoah, Baffert is behind Mor Spirit. This horse ran second and first at Saratoga in the fall and finished runner-up in the Kentucky Jockey Club G2 at Churchill Downs in late November. Baffert has been very vocal about the talented offspring of Eskendereya and his chances at challenging for the Kentucky Derby title and beyond. He should know. He had a front-row seat this year.
