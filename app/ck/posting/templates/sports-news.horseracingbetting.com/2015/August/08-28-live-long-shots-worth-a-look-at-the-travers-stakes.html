Live long shots worth a look at the Travers Stakes



  Tweet

Here are three live long shots worth a look this Saturday when you are betting on horse races


 

The 146th running of the Travers Stakes is headlined by Triple Crown champion American Pharoah, who has given horse handicappers plenty of reasons to back him since winning the Kentucky Derby in the spring.

But with racebooks making you pay a premium for a ticket of American Pharoah – a -500 favorite – there is plenty of value behind Bob Baffert’s prized three year old at the Travers this weekend. Here are three live long shots worth a look this Saturday when you are betting on horse races:

Keen Ice (12/1)
Keen Ice seems to be peaking late in the season. He ran third in the Belmont Stakes, behind American Pharoah and Frosted, who is the second overall favorite at 6/1 odds Saturday, and came in second behind AP in the Haskell Invitational earlier this month. Not only is Keen Ice and veteran jockey Kent Desormeaux a live long shot but there could be plenty of fans at Saratoga Saturday with local ice cream shop, The Dairy Häus, offering free “Keen Ice Cream” to celebrate the Donegal Racing horse this weekend.

Upstart (15/1)
Even with the Travers just hours away, trainer Rick Violette is still not sure if he's putting Upstart in the Travers Stakes field. For now, it looks like he’s going to run and oddsmakers have priced him out among the Top 5 horses at Saratoga. Upstart drew the rail for the Travers Stakes, which jockey Irad Ortiz Jr. and Violette actually like. They will gain ground on the first turn, which might be just the edge they need if American Pharoah doesn’t perform up to his past standards. Upstart thrived at Saratoga last summer before making the leap to graded events.

Smart Transition (20/1)
This horse gets a new rider with Hall of Fame jockey John Velazquez replacing former mount Junior Alvarado. Bettors will have to wait and see if the moves turns out to be a “smart transition” for the Curlin Stakes winner. Velazquez is 1 for 14 in Travers Stakes races, winning aboard Flower Alley in 2005. He’s third in the Saratoga rider standings with 31 wins in New York but is also Smart Transitions' fourth rider in his last seven starts, so who knows how the horse will respond.
