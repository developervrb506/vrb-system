New faces are jumping up the 2018 Kentucky Derby odds



  Tweet

Hot contenders climbing up the board as the Kentucky Derby betting heats up.


We took a glance at the top bets to win the 2018 Kentucky Derby a little over a month ago, and in that short time horse racing oddsmakers have shaken up those futures, with a slew of hot contenders climbing up the board as the Kentucky Derby betting heats up.

Odds-on-favorite Bolt D’oro is still the horse to beat come the first weekend in May, moving from +1,200 to +550 despite finishing third in the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile at the beginning of the month. That race had a huge impact on the Derby futures, and these are the new kids on the block – as it pertains to the 2018 Run For The Roses.

Good Magic (+1,000)
Good Magic took the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile at Del Mar, and instantly vaulted up the Kentucky Derby odds. That was just his third race and his maiden win after two runner-up showings in the Champagne-G1 at Belmont Park in October and at Saratoga in late August. Good Magic exploded down the home stretch of that race and left all contenders in his dust. The Chad Brown-trained two-year-old not only defeated Bolt D’oro but also half-brother Solomini, another product of Curlin, who finished second in the Juvenile. Solomini was price at +3,000 to win the Derby before that race and is now down to +2,000 odds.

Montauk (+1,000)
This horse has just one race – and one win – to his record, but there is a lot of hype building. Montauk caught the attention of racing bettors with a 11 ¼-lengths victory at Belmont Park in early October and many hoped to see the two-year-old compete in the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile. However, trainer Todd Pletcher – another reason why this colt is among the early Derby faves – decided not to run him at Del Mar. He also kept Montauk out of the Nashua on November 5. There has been radio silence when it comes to his upcoming races, so perhaps there is more to these absences than first thought.

Beautiful Shot (+1,500)
This horse recently finished third in the Bob Hope Stakes-G3 at Del Mar on November 11, after winning his first two races at Saratoga in October and at Del Mar in August. He too skipped the Breeders’ Cup Juvenile at the request of trainer Keith Desormeaux. He’s a late-running sprinter but didn’t have the speed to keep pace in the Bob Hope, falling well off the pace behind Greyvitos and Mourinho who were left to battle down the stretch. That lack of stamina may have some racing bettors questioning his chance at the longer distance.

Enticed (+1,500)
He placed third in the Champagne-G1 at Belmont Park in October, finishing behind Good Magic and race winner Firenze Fire. That was his second race after winning at Saratoga in September. He fell off the pace around the midway mark of that race, but battled back to show with a gusty effort down the stretch. The Kiaran McLaughlin-trained colt is chasing down half-brother Bolt D’oro, who comes from his same sire Medaglia d'Oro. It would make for one intriguing storyline if he can continue to climb the 2018 Kentucky Derby odds.

Saxon Warrior (+1,500)
Saxon Warrior is one of four horses sitting at 15/1 to win the 2018 Kentucky Derby and is the top international contender, with famed Irish trainer Aidan O'Brien guiding his path. He’s won all three of his career races, including a victory in the Racing Post Trophy-G1 at Doncaster in late October, and holds some solid value as a relatively unknown in North American racing circles. Some Las Vegas books has Saxon Warrior as big as +8,500 before edging top competition Roaring Lion in the Group 1 Racing Post.
