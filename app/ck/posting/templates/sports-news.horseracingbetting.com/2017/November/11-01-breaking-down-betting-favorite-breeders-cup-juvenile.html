Breaking down the betting favorite for the 2017 Breeder's Cup Juvenile



  Tweet

The Breeder’s Cup Juvenile is one of the first steps


 

The Breeder’s Cup is the unofficial close of the horse racing calendar but in many ways sets the table for next year’s Triple Crown season. The Breeder’s Cup Juvenile is one of the first steps – and perhaps the biggest – in predicting the front runners for the Kentucky Derby in May.

The field is loaded with top two-year-olds for the November 4 race at Del Mar. Here are the oddsmakers’ favorites to run up front in the 2017 Breeder’s Cup Betting Juvenile:

Bolt d’Oro (+120)
This horse has really come out of nowhere the past three months. He broke his maiden in his first race of the season at Del Mar in early August, then won his next two events: the Del Mar Futurity G1 and FrontRunner Stakes G1 in September. This Mick Ruis-trained horse has been training for the Juvenile ever since, looking very impressive in practice sessions at Santa Anita earlier in October. Not only is Bolt d’Oro the favorite to win the November 4 race but is now the early front runner in the 2018 Kentucky Derby Odds.

Free Drop Billy (+500)
This race will be the first travel West for Dale L. Romans’ horse, after racing at Churchill Downs, Saratoga and Keeneland this season. Free Drop Billy caught everyone’s eye with a win at Churchill Downs in June and sandwiched two second-place showings at Saratoga between that victory and an impressive first-place run in the Breeders' Futurity-G1 in early October. Since then, this horse has been stunning in his training at Del Mar, so he should have a great feel for the course come race day.

U S Navy Flag (+600)
This Irish-trained horse has a lot more experience than the top two favorites for the Breeder’s Cup Juvenile. U S Navy Flag has raced 10 times since his first appearance at Naas Racecourse in May and crosses the pond for this international event. Trainer Aidan P. O'Brien has his horse peaking at the right time, winning three straight races heading into the Breeder’s Cup, most recently finishing first in the Dewhurst Stakes-G1 on mid-October. He carries a lower profile into Del Mar due to racing in the UK and it will be interesting how the horse responds to the overseas travel as well as the longer distance on November 4.

Firenze Fire (+800)
This horse has moved up the odds board with a recent victory in the Champagne-G1 at Belmont Park in early October, which was his third victory in four career races. Trainer Jason Servis has spaced out his appearances throughout the season, starting with a win at Monmouth Park in June and rolling into a first-place race in the Sanford G3 and a runner-up effort in the Hopeful G1 at Saratoga in July and September. The 1 1/16-mile distance could be an issue for Firenze Fire, who has yet to go longer than one mile.

The Tabulator (+1,200)
This horse was heavily hyped heading into the fall, and some books had him as the 2018 Kentucky Derby favorite when odds hit the board in late September. He’s won all three of his races this season, most recently taking first at the Iroquois-G3 at Churchill Downs in September. His hype has cooled, thanks in part to reports of bad training sessions heading into the Breeder’s Cup Juvenile . However, The Tabulator does have veteran jockey Jose Valdivia Jr. on board and he’s helped this horse improve with each race in 2017. There could be serious value here, considering how far down the odds board he’s fallen and what this horse is capable of.
