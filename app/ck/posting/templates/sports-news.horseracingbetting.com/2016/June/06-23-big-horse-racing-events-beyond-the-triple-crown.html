Big horse racing events beyond the Triple Crown



  Tweet

Here are four must-wager major horse races still to come in 2016


 

For most causal horse racing fans, the season begins and ends with the Triple Crown events – the Kentucky Derby, Preakness, and Belmont Stakes. But there is so much to the racing calendar than those three high-profile events.

Here are four must-watch, must-wager major horse races still to come in 2016:

Haskell Invitational – July 31
The Haskell doesn’t hold the same clout as it did in 2015 when Triple Crown winner American Pharoah ran and won the annual 1 1/8-mile long stakes race at Monmouth Park. However, this race and its $1 million purse has drawn big-name horses over the year and should have some familiar faces in the gates come late July.

Travers Stakes – August 27
This race is notorious for leaving Triple Crown winners in its wake. It was American Pharoah’s lone loss in 2015 and should remain a challenge for this year’s notables. Saratoga Race Course throws 1 ¼ miles at contenders as well as the summer heat, which opens value across the board. Last year’s winner, Keen Ice, was a 12/1 long shot before race day .

Pacific Classic Stakes – August 30
One of the crown jewels of Del Mar’s racing schedule is for horses 3 years old and up, making it one of the most diverse fields of the summer. Not only do you get some of the Triple Crown notables but you also find past winners and well-known contenders, such as last year’s winner Beholder. The 1 ¼-mile race boasts a $1 million purse and the winner gets an automatic bid in the Breeder’s Cup Classic.

Breeders’ Cup Classic – November 5
The racing season unofficially comes to a close with the Breeders’ Cup weekend, which calls Santa Anita Park home in 2016. The Breeders’ Cup Classic is the main event of the weekend, pitting 3 year olds and up against each other in a field loaded with past Triple Crown winners and contenders and a $5 million purse is up for grabs. The races listed above will help define the field for the Classic, so pay close attention to how horse perform in those qualifying events.
