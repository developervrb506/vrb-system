There’s little doubt who the favorite is for the Preakness Horse Race on May 21, with oddsmakers installing Nyquist as a -175 favorite after his dominant win in the Kentucky Derby earlier this month. Those odds make just about every other horse in the field a large underdog.

However, if you are looking for a big score at Pimlico Race Track in Baltimore later this month, these horses below may just be the live long shot you’re looking for:

Brody’s Cause (+2,000)
With the Preakness field still taking form, Brody’s Cause is sitting at longer odds since horses that finished ahead of him in the Derby are still on the board. But as those trainers make their decisions in the coming weeks, his odds will likely slide down.

He placed seventh in the Kentucky Derby but has shown he has what it takes to run up front, with a win in the Blue Grass Stakes earlier this year. He finished third in the Breeder’s Cup Juvenile last October and edged Exaggerator as a 2 year old at Churchill Downs last September.

Cherry Wine (+2,500)
He sat out the Kentucky Derby after just missing out on qualifying. He placed third in the Blue Grass Stakes in March, but hasn’t raced since. The Dale Romans-trained colt is a lighter horse with great closing speed. The shorter distance at Pimlico may be the perfect set up for Cherry Wine, who trained very well leading up the Derby in hopes that he could slide into the field after a late scratch. But, since that didn’t happen, he should be plenty fresh for this fast and furious race.

Laoban (+2,500)
Laoban didn’t run in the Kentucky Derby after just missing out on points but has the speed to be very disruptive in the Preakness. With this being half a furlong shorter, it really does become a sprint to the finish and if some of the favorites try to run with this maiden, they could end up blowing themselves up early on. If he can set the pace and keep it up, he’ll challenge any horse in the field.
