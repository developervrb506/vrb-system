Early look at the Breeders’ Cup Classic favorites



  Tweet

Triple Crown winner American Pharoah completed horse racing’s Grand Slam


 

The Breeders’ Cup Betting Classic is still a few months out with the very best in horse racing heading to Santa Anita on November 5. And while the Classic doesn’t have the same hype as last year, when Triple Crown winner American Pharoah completed horse racing’s Grand Slam, there is still a stacked field of potential winners. Here’s a look at the early favorites to win the Breeders’ Cup Classic:

California Chrome +400
The 2014 Kentucky Derby and Preakness winner hasn’t slowed down with age. He’s swept all three 2016 races, winning the San Pasqual Stakes-G2, the Handicap at Meydan, and the Dubai World Cup-G1. Now, that last race came way back in March, but California Chrome looked good in his practice run for the San Diego Handicap at Del Mar this past week. Should he win there, this price will likely get trimmed significantly. He finished third at the Breeders’ Cup Classic in 2014 at Santa Anita.

Beholder +500
The six-year-old mare is still among the fastest and most-beloved horses in the sport. She didn’t run in last year’s Classic, snuffing out which would have been an ultimate showdown with American Pharoah. In 2016, she’s won the Adoration Stakes-G3 and Vanity-G1 – both at Santa Anita – and is expected to race in the Clement L. Hirsch Stakes and have a potential matchup with California Chrome in the Pacific Classic on August 20 – a race she won by 8½ lengths last year.

Frosted +600
Frosted may not have the name power or championships to his name, but he remains one of the most consistent horses in the sport. He traveled overseas and won the Al Maktoum Challenge back in February and took the Metropolitan Handicap – AKA the Met Mile – in June at Belmont Park. This horse stood toe-to-toe with Amercian Pharoah in most events in 2015, finishing fourth in the Derby, second in the Belmont and third in the Travers Stakes before finishing seventh in the Breeders’ Cup Classic. He's used to stiff competition.

Other notables:

Songbird +800
Nyquist +1,000
Creator +1,200
Exaggerator +1,400
