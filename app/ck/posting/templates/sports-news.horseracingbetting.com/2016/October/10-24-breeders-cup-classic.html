Breeder’s Cup Classic: Live Long Shot To Watch



  Tweet

The oddsmakers’ have their favorites


 

The 2016 Breeder’s Cup calls Santa Anita Park home this November and the main event of that weekend is the Breeder’s Cup Classic, pitting some of the top horse around the globe against each other.

The oddsmakers’ have their favorites but if you’re looking to hit it big with some breeders cup betting, look no further than these three live long shots on November 4.

Connect (+2,200)
Connect was behind Exaggerator on the Breeder’s Cup Classic board before he retired from racing, and you still may be able to score this 3-year-old at bigger odds. He’s coming off a huge win at the Pennsylvania Derby and also won at the Curlin at Saratoga earlier this year. He cashed in as a 10/1 underdog in Pennsylvania, so coming out of nowhere is his style. He’s never run at Santa Anita but brings a ton of momentum into this race, should he decide to run.

Dortmund (+2,500)
This horse would be better known if not for American Pharoah’s dominance in 2015. He ran well against the Triple Crown winner and continues to be a strong contender as a 4-year-old. He’s finished second twice and third once in his three 2016 races, most recently a runner-up finish at Awesome Again G1 at Santa Anita. He’s very familiar with this track and seems to step up to his competition, always making him a threat heading into the home stretch.

Frosted (+1,500)
Frosted was another horse left in the wake of American Pharoah last year, finishing fourth at the Kentucky Derby and second in the Belmont Stakes. Since then, this Kiaran P. McLaughlin-trained 4-year-old has been globetrotting with a wins at the Al Maktoum Challenge in Dubai, the Metropolitan Handicap, and the Whitney Handicap. Most recently, Frosted finished third in the Woodward G1 at Saratoga. However, while that resume is impressive, he’s yet to win at the 10-furlong distance which the Classic is run.
