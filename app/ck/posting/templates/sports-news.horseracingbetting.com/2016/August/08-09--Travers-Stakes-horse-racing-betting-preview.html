Travers Stakes horse racing betting preview



  Tweet

The field is starting to fill up with notable names built during the Triple Crown campaign


 

The top 3-year-olds in the sport keep the summer horse handicapping schedule rolling with a stop in Saratoga for the Travers Stakes on August 27. The field is starting to fill up with notable names built during the Triple Crown campaign. Here’s a look at the oddsmakers’ favorites to finish first when gates open for the 1 ¼-mile event later this month:

Exaggerator 2/1
The 2016 Preakness winner turned in an impressive victory at the Haskell Invitational last month. Exaggerator came from last place to fight off Kentucky Derby champ Nyquist and challengers American Freedom and Sunny Ridge, blowing past them down the stretch for a 1 1/2-length win at Monmouth Park. However, there has been one edge the J. Keith Desormeaux-trained horse has had in most of its wins: poor track conditions. Exaggerator loves to run in the slop, so his odds for the Travers Stakes could jump around depending on the forecast in late August.

Connect 4/1
Connect doesn’t have the Triple Crown resume that some of the other contenders at the Travers boast but he did turn in a big win at the Curlin Stakes last month, edging favorite Gift Box – a fellow Chad Brown stablemate. Connect was very impressive on the track at Saratoga, especially putting distance between he and his challengers down the home stretch, and that should carry over to the Travers Stakes. The win at the Curlin was his third straight and he holds great value at these odds thanks to be a lesser-known horse.


Nyquist 5/1
The 2016 Kentucky Derby winner will be out for revenge at the Travers, having lost to Exaggerator in their past two meetings. However, the Travers Stakes has historically been unkind to Kentucky Derby winners with a reputation as the “Graveyard of Champions”. Last year, Triple Crown winner American Pharoah lost his only race in the Travers, opening up value beyond the notables. Nyquist finished third at the Preakness and fourth at the Haskell, so the shine could be off the Derby champ despite the short odds. Some oddsmakers even have American Freedom ahead of Nyquist on the the board at 9/2 odds.
