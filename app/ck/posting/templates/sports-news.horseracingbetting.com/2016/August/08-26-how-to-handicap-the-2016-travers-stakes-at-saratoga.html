How to handicap the 2016 Travers Stakes at Saratoga



  Tweet

Here are a couple factors you’ll want to handicap before putting your money down


 

Late-summer in Saratoga leads up to the Travers Stakes on August 27 and the opportunity for some horse racing bets. Some of the top 3-year-olds in the world will step into the gates for the 1 ¼-mile race with a $1,250,000 purse up for grabs. If you’re looking to bet on this always exciting race, here are a couple factors you’ll want to handicap before putting your money down:

Graveyard of Champions
Saratoga has a reputation for spoiling championship runs. Last year, American Pharoah – the first Triple Crown winner since 1978 – got his first taste of defeat in the Travers, losing to Keen Ice. That stigma should at least have horse racing fans considering a flyer beyond the oddsmakers’ favorites. Heading into race week, Exaggerator is a 2/1 favorite with American Freedom and Connect both at 4/1.

If you’re looking to grab some green at the “graveyard” perhaps look to Gift Box (8/1). He’s never finished worse than third – with two wins - and has run twice at Saratoga already in the past two summers, most recently placing second behind Connect at the Curlin in late July.

Watch the weather
Upstate New York can see some wild weather at this time of year, and with this being an especially hot summer there have been some crazy thunderstorms and downpours in the area. If the raceday forecast is calling for rain, you may want to dive deeper into how these horses respond to the slop. Race favorite Exaggerator has already proven he’s at his best in the worst conditions with huge wins in the mud, most recently at the Haskell.If you see rain in the forecast, you might want to grab him before the odds adjust.

Another weather factor horse bettors should watch for is the heat and humidity. It can be extremely humid at Saratoga and that makes horses susceptible to washing out - sweating profusely - before the race even begins. If you can catch the live feeds or follow the pre-race parades, keep an eye out for those horses acting up and looking washy in those minutes before the horn sounds. They might not have much left in the tank if they can't handle the heat.
